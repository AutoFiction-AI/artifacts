# Ash Under Gold Leaf

## Primary Narrative Engine
Combination engine:
- Primary: literary fiction
- Co-engines: character study + family mystery
- Secondary engine: love story

Consequence model for this outline:
- Chapters are driven mainly by informational, emotional, and relational consequences (identity shifts, trust fractures, sobriety setbacks, institutional exclusion).
- External pressure exists (museum politics, legal control of archives), but the plot does not escalate into thriller mechanics.
- Intimacy scenes are treated as narrative events that change leverage and vulnerability, not decorative interludes.

## Chapter Plan (18 Chapters)

1. **Chapter 1 - Intake Week**
Objective: Marisol secures her residency footing and access to Salvador Rivas, the dying painter.
Conflict: The museum wants a celebratory legacy poem cycle; Salvador insists on private interviews and refuses curation control.
Consequence: Marisol accepts his terms, tying her project to his hidden account of her family.
Texture: Institutional setup, high dialogue, compressed scenes.

2. **Chapter 2 - The First Contradiction**
Objective: Establish whether Salvador is credible.
Conflict: Salvador claims Marisol's martyr-father story is fabricated, then retreats behind pain medication and provocation.
Consequence: Marisol chooses investigation over dismissal and reports the destabilization to her sponsor instead of numbing out.
Texture: Two-scene pressure chamber; verbal combat and craving management.

3. **Chapter 3 - All Souls Supper**
Objective: Test family memory without direct accusation.
Conflict: Gloria and Carina enforce the household script whenever Tomas's death is mentioned.
Consequence: Marisol spots a hidden photo linking Gloria and Salvador years before she was born.
Texture: Quiet domestic chapter; long meal, subtext, low dialogue velocity.

4. **Chapter 4 - Underpainting**
Objective: Examine Salvador's triptych with conservator Ada Park.
Conflict: Access protocols and Ada's professional boundaries limit what can be shared.
Consequence: Infrared images reveal a painted-over male figure marked "T.V.," shifting the mystery from rumor to evidence.
Texture: Information-turn chapter; tactile studio detail, little confrontation.

5. **Chapter 5 - Red Hook Inventory**
Objective: Verify the "T.V." timeline through shipping logs.
Conflict: Union gatekeeping, missing boxes, and institutional amnesia frustrate retrieval.
Consequence: Marisol proves Tomas worked the museum night before his supposed protest death.
Texture: Slow-burn discovery; procedural movement without climax.

6. **Chapter 6 - Parking Lot Confession**
Objective: Confront Gloria before the story calcifies again.
Conflict: Gloria preempts her with a partial truth: Tomas died of overdose, not state violence.
Consequence: Marisol relapses after 30 sober months, then conceals it.
Texture: High-intensity confrontation followed by private collapse.

7. **Chapter 7 - Day One Chip**
Objective: Contain relapse fallout and avoid spiraling.
Conflict: Shame and self-mythology make honesty feel worse than drinking.
Consequence: She publicly restarts sobriety at AA and loses a teaching opportunity she missed while drinking.
Texture: Aftermath and inventory; body-level consequence over plot advancement.

8. **Chapter 8 - Solvent and Skin**
Objective: Rebuild trust with Ada.
Conflict: Marisol fears intimacy will expose her instability; Ada refuses to be used as an informant.
Consequence: They become physically intimate, and Marisol discloses the relapse, deepening mutual stakes.
Texture: Quiet-to-intimate arc; emotional tenderness with explicit relational turn.

9. **Chapter 9 - Roof Garden Tape**
Objective: Record Salvador's full testimony.
Conflict: Salvador reveals he may be Marisol's biological father; Marisol cannot separate confession from manipulation.
Consequence: She publishes an accusatory poem online, triggering a museum reputational crisis.
Texture: Two-scene spike: revelation then impulsive public action.

10. **Chapter 10 - Suspension**
Objective: Preserve her residency and access.
Conflict: Museum leadership, donors, and legal counsel close ranks after her post.
Consequence: Stipend frozen, archive access revoked, and Ada is ordered into professional distance.
Texture: Protagonist acted upon by institutional force.

11. **Chapter 11 - Municipal Record Room**
Objective: Build an independently verifiable timeline.
Conflict: Sealed records, clerical hostility, and Marisol's fear of what paperwork will confirm.
Consequence: Certified documents show overdose death and Salvador as witness, proving decades of fabrication.
Texture: Information-driven chapter with low dialogue and documentary rhythm.

12. **Chapter 12 - Family Hearing**
Objective: Force a full accounting at dinner.
Conflict: Gloria defends the lie as survival; Carina accuses Marisol of turning pain into career material.
Consequence: Carina throws Marisol out, splitting the family into hostile camps.
Texture: Domestic tribunal; long scene, escalating interruptions, no external action.

13. **Chapter 13 - Catalogue Draft**
Objective: Write Salvador's essay before he dies.
Conflict: Marisol must choose between revenge narrative and accountable complexity.
Consequence: She drafts a version that includes Tomas's addiction, Gloria's coercive choices, and her own relapse.
Texture: Preparation/dread chapter; writing table tension, deferred climax.

14. **Chapter 14 - Saturday with Leo**
Objective: Re-enter family life without arguing the case.
Conflict: Awkward co-presence with Carina and her son Leo keeps every sentence loaded.
Consequence: Marisol learns Gloria pawned her wedding ring for Marisol's first rehab, reframing maternal motive.
Texture: Quiet chapter with humor and ordinary tasks (homework, cheap takeout, bus ride).

15. **Chapter 15 - Estate Hold**
Objective: Secure legal right to use Salvador's testimony after his death.
Conflict: Estate counsel blocks release; Ada risks her job by sharing conservation evidence.
Consequence: Marisol obtains hard proof but Ada is placed on administrative leave.
Texture: Information-and-cost turn; consequence lands on secondary character.

16. **Chapter 16 - Wake**
Objective: Refuse myth-making at Salvador's funeral rites.
Conflict: Community mourners and Gloria both demand a cleaner story than truth allows.
Consequence: Marisol reads a raw poem naming contradiction; Gloria walks out and the split becomes public.
Texture: Ritual chapter; collective space, high emotional density.

17. **Chapter 17 - Off-Season Boardwalk**
Objective: Decide what to publish and what to protect.
Conflict: Gloria reveals Tomas chose the martyr lie to preserve benefits and leave Marisol a less shameful inheritance.
Consequence: Marisol rejects total exposé and commits to a hybrid book that preserves facts while honoring consent boundaries.
Texture: Extended two-hander; confession, ethical negotiation, winter stillness.

18. **Chapter 18 - Small Reading**
Objective: Live forward without the martyr script.
Conflict: Career fallout, fragile sobriety, and uncertain love resist tidy resolution.
Consequence: At a neighborhood reading, Marisol chooses language of survival over sanctification; she and Ada restart cautiously.
Texture: Measured denouement; modest stakes, earned quiet.

## Parallel Drafting Notes
- All chapters have explicit state change; no chapter ends in neutral stasis.
- Chapter IDs in all artifacts use `chapter_01` through `chapter_18`.
- Distinct mechanisms intentionally distributed: slow-burn discovery (5), acted-upon institutional force (10), aftermath/inventory (7), preparation/dread (13), information-turns (4, 11, 15), and quiet baseline chapters (3, 8, 14, 18).
