# Chapter 15

The valley smelled different in the early morning. Cooler air pushed the contamination stink down close to the ground and thinned it enough that Maren could almost pretend the ozone-sweet rot wasn't there. Almost. The hum from the three dungeon mouths had banked low with the night, not gone quiet so much as dropped into the earth. He'd slept badly on the thin bedroll Kael had lent him, the entity pressing against the inside of his skull in slow rhythmic surges like another body trying to breathe through his ribs, and when he'd crawled out before sunrise the Crook had looked peaceful in the ugly way contaminated places could look peaceful. Quest-light lay in the low spots with a faint shimmer, pale as moonlight on standing water.

He was east of the camp now, on his knees in cold dirt, trying to remember the second containment pass Thessa had drilled into him the afternoon before. The ceramic bowl rested against his thigh in its loose cloth wrapping because he'd carried it out with him automatically. He wasn't using it. He was only trying to get the order of the motions right before Thessa came looking and informed him, with complete justice, that he'd forgotten half of what she'd taught him overnight. His left wrist kept collapsing. His fingers kept stiffening at the wrong moment. Every time it happened he had to start again.

Behind him, the camp was waking up in pieces. Petra had the fire going and three clay jars spread out on the table beside her. She worked by smell, nose close to the mouths, making small dissatisfied noises when one sample had turned overnight. Her bad left hip pulled her sideways when she walked, and her fingers were stained the same dark brown as Thessa's from years of handling what other people avoided touching. Ruald sat on his upturned bucket near the eastern lean-to, chewing root and rewrapping the cloth strips he used for his own version of the containment pass. The strips were grey-brown from repeated exposure and smelled faintly of burnt sugar. He'd mentioned once, in the middle of an unrelated complaint, that his wife folded them for him when he came home, matching the edges exactly.

Denna was behind the second lean-to, quiet as she'd been the night before, scratching a fresh notch into the leather tally tied to her belt. She ate with her left hand and wrote with her right. That was one of the only things Maren knew about her. Kael had just come out of the western shelter, still pulling her coat straight from sleep, and was crouched by the water barrel washing yesterday's contamination grit off her hands. Hale was farther south on first-light watch where the valley rose toward the old residue beds. From here he was mostly a dark shape against the grey, standing still in the way only watchful men stood still, like movement was something to be spent carefully.

*Four hostiles approaching from the northwest ridge.*

Aldric's voice came fast and flat. Under that clipped delivery sat something worse than urgency. Recognition.

*They are in full combat loadout. Caul is leading them. Two melee fighters and one support caster.*

Maren came up off his knees so fast his ankle folded under him. He grabbed the bowl by its wrapping on reflex and caught himself against the trunk of a dead birch. "How far?"

*Quarter mile. Descending now. The caster is already building a targeting overlay. They have the camp.*

The bowl knocked against his wrist. He'd known Caul would come. Derry had given him the direction and Caul was a tracker, patient in the way men got patient when patience was the only thing between them and the grief underneath. Maren had still thought he'd have longer than this. A day, maybe two. Enough time to turn instruction into something less embarrassing than whatever he was currently doing in the dirt.

He ran.

"Heroes," he said as he crossed the camp. "Four. Northwest ridge. They've got a targeting overlay on us."

Petra's half-humming stopped. Ruald spat his root into the dust and stood. Denna straightened with the leather tally still in one hand. Kael's head snapped toward the ridge.

Thessa came out of the largest lean-to already awake enough to be angry. Her hair was flattened on one side from sleep and her stained hands were empty. That meant she'd put something down the second she heard his feet.

"How many?"

"Four. Ranger, two melee, one caster. The ranger's Caul."

The recalculation happened in her face all at once. "Leave the shelters. Carry only what matters. Petra, take the jars. Ruald, leave the bucket. Denna, get moving. Kael, call Hale in. We're going south to the third mouth."

"We can't outrun a hero party," Ruald said. The cloth strips hung from one hand, limp and dirty. "Thessa, we're not combat-classified. We don't have any way to"

"I'm not asking you to fight. The contamination around the third mouth is deep enough to blur target locks." She looked at Maren then, and that look had no warmth in it at all. "You're why they're here."

"Yeah."

"Then you stay and buy us the time."

It wasn't a question. Thessa turned and started moving the camp with the clipped, hard precision she used when work had gone bad enough that discussion was a waste of breath. Petra swept the clay jars against her chest. Denna snatched up her pack and the tally strip vanished into a pocket. Kael was already yelling for Hale down the slope. Ruald bent once for his satchel, missed the buckle, swore under his breath, and tried again.

*They will not make clean distance,* Vess said. *The targeting overlay covers the full valley.*

*Confirmed. The sin-eaters are flagged as non-combatant civilians.* Aldric paused. *Collateral authorization has been granted. The bounty's threat threshold overrides civilian protection.*

"What does that mean in actual words?"

*It means they are allowed to kill anyone between them and you.*

The first arrow hit the eastern lean-to's support pole with a flat crack and the whole side of the shelter sagged at once. Maren never heard the bow. He heard the impact. He heard Petra suck in a breath. Then a second arrow buried itself in the dirt three feet from Ruald's boots.

"Move," Maren shouted. "Ruald, move, don't—"

The two melee heroes came over the north lip of the camp at a run. One tall with a polearm. One shorter, fast, dual blades out. Maren didn't have the data for them, only the shapes and the speed and the way his own body recognized trouble before his mind could sort it. Aldric's training surged into his muscles. Vess's killing instinct came up cold along his spine. By the time he decided to put himself between the fighters and the retreating sin-eaters, his feet had already done it.

The tall one reached him first. He felt the air move before he properly understood the sweep. Low strike for the legs. His body had already stepped out of it, half turned, hand clamped to the shaft to shove the arc past his hip. The weapon's weight jarred up through his wrists. Leather oil. Road sweat. Something sharp and herbal in the woman's armor padding.

*Third-tier melee. Polearm specialist. Inside her reach and the advantage disappears.*

He was already inside it. His elbow crashed into the side of her head above the ear guard. She staggered.

The second fighter came from the right with dual blades out, young enough that his face still had some softness left in it. Maren only caught that much before his body took over again. Forearm against the flat of one blade. Pivot outside the other. Knee into the boy's stance hard enough to make it buckle.

He was fighting and not fighting. The fragments operated his body while Maren occupied it, dragged through the thing by sensation and weight and smell. No damage numbers. No threat markers. Just the shock of impact in his bones, contaminated dirt giving under his boots, metal scraping leather.

Petra screamed, high and ragged and stripped of anything that sounded like fight. Maren's head turned while the rest of him was still engaged with the boy. Petra was down near the third lean-to, clay jars smashed around her, dark residue spreading into the discolored ground. The arrow was lodged below her left shoulder blade. She was trying to crawl and her bad hip dragged uselessly behind her.

*Caul. Northwest ridge. Sixty yards and cycling another shot.*

The second arrow took Ruald in the throat.

He'd turned back when Petra went down. That was the whole stupid human thing of it. He'd turned back for a woman he'd worked beside for years in a job nobody respected, and the broadhead found the gap under his jaw. He sat down hard as if a stool had been yanked out from under him. His hands went to his neck. The cloth strips spilled from the satchel, grey-brown in the dirt, still carrying that burnt sugar smell and the memory of a wife Maren would never meet.

*Left,* Vess said. *The polearm fighter is up again.*

His body spun. The overhead chop came down. Maren ducked under it and drove his fist into the woman's diaphragm. She folded. Something in her knee gave with a branch-snap sound and she went to the ground clutching it.

The dual-blade boy backed off three steps too fast. Maren heard his breathing, ragged and rising, the breath of somebody whose training had not prepared him for a target the System couldn't classify. Whatever overlay he was looking at, it showed a hole where Maren stood.

"Denna's down." Kael's voice came from the south side of the camp, flat with shock. "The caster hit her with some kind of pulse. She just dropped."

Three sin-eaters. Three people who had been here doing the work that kept farmland from turning poisonous and children from growing up in residue. Dead because Maren had brought his bounty into their camp.

*The caster is repositioning east,* Aldric said. *She is trying to establish a precision lock on you.*

Maren looked south. Thessa was fifty yards out, moving fast. Kael was with her. Hale was higher on the slope, waving them toward the older contamination and then turning back long enough to make sure they kept moving. Behind Maren, the polearm woman was on the ground with her ruined knee, the boy was retreating uphill toward Caul, and the caster was somewhere he couldn't see because he couldn't see anything the System built.

*Maren.* Aldric's voice had lost its report-cadence. *The caster's next pulse will establish a range lock. If she completes the scan, they will be able to strike through the contamination.*

"How long?"

*Thirty seconds. Perhaps less.*

Maren looked past the little practice patch near his knees to the residue pool between the first and second dungeon mouths, a shallow lowland full of years of accumulated contamination, cold-glowing and dense enough that even he could read its shape. The smell coming off it was layered and heavy, fresh ozone over old sweetness over the sour metal stink of residue that had been sitting in the same dirt long enough to change the dirt.

*No,* Aldric said at once. *That is a nexus-grade pool. You consumed a fraction of a minor pool under supervision and it nearly broke you.*

"If I don't do it, the caster gets the lock and everybody on that slope dies."

*You cannot control that much contamination.*

"I'm not trying to control it."

He was already running. The bowl thumped against his side. His boots slid on the bad ground. Inside him the entity pressed outward as if it could feel what he was about to do and had reached for it before he had.

Vess didn't answer. Her silence was its own verdict.

---

He dropped at the edge of the pool and yanked the wrapped bowl free because it was in the way. He let it fall beside him in the dirt. The contamination hummed through his trousers and into his shins, not the background hum of the valley but a local vibration with its own pitch. The glow was cold. He could feel it on the skin of his hands like the pressure of someone staring.

He used the second opening, the one Thessa had called reckless and emergency-only and too easy to do wrong. The opening did not feel like skill. It felt like stopping the body's natural flinch. Like unlatching something in himself that every sane person kept shut.

The contamination came in like floodwater, nothing like yesterday's supervised pull. It was cold and massive, more pressure than his body had any language for. His chest filled with something that wasn't air and wasn't liquid but had the weight of both. Ozone coated the back of his tongue. Copper. A sweetness on the turn toward rot. His teeth ached all the way up into the roots. The bones behind his eyes felt squeezed inward by hands that didn't exist.

*The targeting overlay is degrading,* Aldric said, voice gone distant. *Their interface is flickering around the consumption point.*

More. He pulled more. The contamination rushed at the opening as if it had been waiting for one. That was the part nobody had explained to him. It wanted to go somewhere. It poured toward him with a horrible eagerness and the pool's glow dimmed as the residue went into him.

Inside his skull the unclassified thing responded at once.

It surged.

His bones started vibrating at a frequency that didn't belong to any human skeleton. Every muscle in his back clenched at once, not like a cramp but like something had reached into him and tried to close a fist. His knee slammed down on the bowl where he'd dropped it. Ceramic cracked sharp under him and a shard punched through his trouser leg into the skin above his kneecap. The pain barely registered.

*Maren, stop,* Vess said. For the first time since he'd known her, the flatness in her voice had split. *The entity is expanding beyond your containment threshold.*

He couldn't stop. The consumption had momentum now. The pool was feeding into him faster than he could regulate and the entity was drinking from the same channel, unfolding inside his head, pressing against his consciousness like something trying to stand in a room that had been built too low for it.

*The System sees it.* Aldric sounded nothing like himself. *It is not registering a threat class. It is registering a void, a gap in the architecture.*

The pool went dark without going empty, as if the light had been eaten out of it and left the residue behind. The last of the contamination slammed into him, the channel snapped shut, and Maren was on his hands and knees over dead soil retching uselessly because it wasn't in his stomach. It was everywhere else.

The hum from the dungeon mouths jumped an octave in a single hard step, going from the low furnace-bass he'd been hearing for two days to something high enough to scrape his sinuses raw. The air temperature dropped with it. One breath and the early-autumn morning was gone, replaced by the damp cold of a cellar.

*Local reclassification in progress,* Aldric said, and now the old formal cadence was back because fear had driven him into it. *Crook Valley redesignation. Threat classification: Critical. Contamination index: Extreme. Authorized response: full containment protocol.*

"What does that mean?"

*It means the System is treating this valley as a breach site. Every entity inside the perimeter is now a valid threat target. Including Caul's party.*

---

The quest-light came up first. It burst through the soil in hard bright runners, cold threads racing over the discolored ground with the speed of roots finding water. In seconds the valley floor was webbed in it, pale branching lines connecting and reconnecting until the whole low ground looked netted in visible structure.

Then the contamination spiked. The smell changed before anything else did, sweetness turning acrid, the air going thick enough that every breath had weight. The nearest dungeon mouth began giving off shapes.

They came out wrong, with too many joints and limbs bending where living bodies didn't. Dog-sized things lit from inside with the same cold light running through the valley floor, threat creatures the System had made for this exact correction, an immune response given bodies.

The first one oriented toward Maren, stopped, and turned its malformed head toward the northwest ridge instead.

*They are not prioritizing you,* Aldric said quietly. *The entity makes you unreadable. They are going for anything the System can classify.*

"Caul's people."

*Yes.*

From the ridge Caul shouted, voice gone raw enough that Maren almost didn't recognize it. "Fall back to the ridge. All units, disengage the target. We have..." Something struck something else with a sound that wasn't flesh and wasn't stone. "What is this? This isn't standard."

The polearm woman screamed.

*She's down,* Vess said. *The creatures reached her before she cleared the zone.*

Maren got to his feet. Everything about his body felt wrong now, too heavy and badly balanced, as if the contamination inside him had been packed unevenly through his bones. The cracked bowl lay in the dirt at his feet with the cloth half caught beneath it. He snatched it up by reflex.

More creatures were coming out of all three mouths. The valley floor started to crawl.

The young dual-blade fighter was crying now, wet and ragged between the clang of his blades against bodies the System itself had generated. Caul said, "No." Then again, louder and with something in it breaking. "No."

*He's reading the order,* Aldric said. *He can see his own party tagged inside the response parameters.*

Caul stopped fighting in Maren's direction. Whatever pursuit had brought him here was over. He turned on the ridge and started cutting down the creatures closing on the boy behind him.

Thessa appeared at Maren's side carrying one of Petra's clay jars. He hadn't heard her approach. She moved through contaminated ground the way other people moved through their own kitchens, feet finding the safe patches without hesitation. She uncapped the jar and poured a thick dark ribbon across the dirt between them and the nearest creature. Its legs hit the old residue and lost traction. It skidded, went over on too many elbows, and Thessa drove it sideways with the flat of her boot into a deeper puddle where the thing started dissolving from the joints inward.

"Three jars left," she said. "That buys us minutes, not many."

"Who got out?"

"Kael and Hale are over the south ridge already." Her tone didn't shift. "Petra's dead. Ruald's dead. Denna's dead. The caster pulsed Denna down before the reclassification. Caul's arrows took the other two."

That was the whole Crook accounted for. Thessa, Kael, and Hale were alive. Petra with the jars, Ruald with the root in his cheek and the strips his wife folded, and Denna with the tally at her belt were dead. Three were alive and three were dead. The numbers sat in Maren's body like swallowed stones, and he did not say *I'm sorry* because there wasn't room for words that small.

"Can we get out past the third mouth?"

"Yes, if you step where I step and don't improvise." She was moving already, pouring a second jar in a broken line ahead of them. "The old contamination on the south slope throws enough noise to mask heat signatures. Stay off the quest-light. If it touches skin, it tags you."

Maren followed. The consumed residue made his legs thick and unreliable, every step a negotiation. Behind them the valley had turned unrecognizable, all cold lines and wrong-jointed movement and the hum pushing against his eardrums hard enough to hurt.

*Caul has abandoned pursuit,* Vess said. *He is holding the ridge for the surviving melee fighter. The caster is down.*

Maren stopped at the foot of the south slope. Below him the valley seethed with light. Ahead, Thessa had reached the old contamination band. He could hear Caul's sword carrying over the noise in a steady working rhythm, efficient and stubborn.

*He has a route out,* Aldric said softly. *The perimeter is open on the northwest side. He could leave if he chose to.*

"He won't leave the boy."

*No,* Aldric said. *He will not.*

Thessa turned and looked back at him from ten yards up the slope. "Move. There's nothing you can do for him from here."

She was right. Maren hated that she was right, but that did not make her less right. He climbed.

The south slope was steep and slick with old residue worked so deep into the soil it had stopped feeling accidental. The smell changed as they rose, less raw ozone, more earth gone strange over time. Thessa never broke stride. Her feet knew this terrain.

They crested the ridge. The far side dropped into scrub and thin trees already starting to turn for autumn. The air out there was cleaner by degrees, which was enough to feel like a different world.

Maren turned once before following her down. From the ridge he could see the whole valley. The camp was gone inside the web of quest-light. On the far side Caul still held the higher ground with the boy crouched behind him. The creatures were beginning to thin on the ridge and bunch lower on the valley floor.

*The boy is alive,* Aldric said. *The response is already narrowing toward the center of the breach.*

Three of them, Maren thought. The sentence wouldn't stay thought-sized. It came out of his mouth. "Three of them. Because of me."

Thessa stood beside him long enough to answer. "Yes."

She started down the far slope. Maren followed. The cracked bowl knocked against his hip through the cloth at every step, and behind him the valley kept humming.
