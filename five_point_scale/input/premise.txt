In late 2022, a broke painter watches his last reliable client — children's book covers — replace him with Midjourney, and decides on a plan so stupid it might be brilliant: he will teach himself to code, infiltrate one of the data-labeling companies that train every major AI image generator, and poison the training data from the inside. The novel is a dark workplace comedy about a man who is genuinely terrible at LeetCode but too stubborn and too angry to quit. He scrapes through a technical interview he has no business passing, lands on the image-evaluation team at a Scale AI-like company in early 2023, and discovers two things: first, that his trained painter's eye makes him the best evaluator on the floor, because he can spot anatomical errors and lighting failures that nobody else noticend second, that his coworkers are exactly the people his sabotage would destroy — fellow artists and gig workers holding onto the only job their skills still qualify them for. As he rises through the company during the 2023 AI image-generation arms race, the novel tracks his double life: performing excellence at work he intends to undermine, building genuine friendships with people he's planning to betray, and watching his own creative identity dissolve into rubrics and metrics until the day he opens a training batch and finds his own unsold paintings being rated by strangers on a five-point scale. When he finally executes the attack in early 2024 — systematically corrupting data so that flagship models produce the six-fingered, melted-face grotesqueries the public already blames on AI's inherent limitations — the fallout is nothing he predicted: the industry doubles down on hiring more labelers instead of returning to human artists, his coworkers face a company-wide investigation, and the outputs becviral aesthetic.
