## Selected Premise
In a city where the rich lease custom-grown bodies for weekends, a claims adjuster for a discount body printer spends her days matching dents, fluids, and borrowed memories to the right customers. When bargain models start coming back with sex lives, childhoods, and injuries that belong to no one on file, she opens a black-market repair desk for the poor and learns the factory is building people out of overlap.

## Selection Rationale
`candidate_06` won because it has the best combination of durable novel engine and expansion capacity. The claims workflow can generate a long run of concrete scenes without feeling schematic, the protagonist sits at a rich class crossroads, and the premise naturally widens from funny and filthy casework into labor exploitation, intimacy, fraud, and personhood. It is original without feeling like a one-sentence stunt.

Against the other finalists, it kept more plates in the air at once. `candidate_02` has beauty, civic reach, and emotional patience, but its checkpoint pattern is easier to over-regularize. `candidate_10` has real durability and humane weight, but less ignition and less aesthetic stretch. `candidate_03` is lively and legitimately unruly, yet its scandal engine looks somewhat lighter in consequence range. `candidate_12` represents genuine risk-taking rather than empty sensationalism because the sex, class performance, and marsh appetite are integral to the premise engine; it still lost because the characters are more exposed to satiric flattening. The shortlist also guarded against empty prestige-safety: the winner was not chosen for respectability, and the stranger, comic, erotic, and lower-intensity alternatives were kept in active contention until the end.
