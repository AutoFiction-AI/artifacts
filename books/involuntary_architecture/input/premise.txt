Post-war Dramione. Magic is dying; the only records that might explain it are in the Malfoy library. Draco grants access if Hermione helps with his waking Dark Mark. The Manor shifts like Blue Prince — never the same layout twice, rooms opening only when both are present, the house testing what's between them. The drawing room — where she was tortured, where he watched — appears when she's closest to letting her guard down. The Manor amplifies the connection between them: she starts feeling his Mark when she's not examining it, his pain and proximity involuntary in her awareness; he hears the house respond to her in ways it never responded to him. Neither knows if what's growing is real or if the Manor is engineering a bond because it needs them. That doubt is the central tension of the romance.

Draco: careful, dry, precise, isolated, touch-starved — the Mark examinations are the first time someone has touched him with care in years. Hermione: competence as armor, divorced from Ron, the Mudblood scar she never lets anyone see. Romance first, dual POV, slow burn, enemies to lovers, forced proximity, intellectual equals, mutual pining, hurt/comfort, angst with hopeful ending. The Malfoy/Granger to Draco/Hermione transition is a milestone. The moment he sees the scar is a turning point. Narcissa knows more about the Manor than Draco does. Cast: Harry, Ginny, Theo Nott, Ron. First kiss late, major rupture final third, book 1 of series. Dark, intense, emotionally devastating. Manacled meets Remain Nameless meets Blue Prince.
