# Chapter 15

The valley smelled different in the early morning. Cooler air pushed the contamination stink down close to the ground and thinned it enough that Maren could almost pretend the ozone-sweet rot was not there. Almost. The hum from the three dungeon mouths had banked low with the night, not gone quiet so much as dropped into the earth. He had slept badly on the thin bedroll Kael had lent him, the entity pressing against the inside of his skull in slow rhythmic surges like another body trying to breathe through his ribs, and when he had crawled out before sunrise the Crook had looked peaceful in the ugly way contaminated places could look peaceful. Quest-light lay in the low spots with a faint shimmer, pale as moonlight on standing water.

He was east of the camp now, on his knees in cold dirt, trying to remember the second containment pass Thessa had drilled into him the afternoon before. The ceramic bowl rested against his thigh in its loose cloth wrapping because he had carried it out with him automatically. He was not using it. He was only trying to get the order of the motions right before Thessa came looking and informed him, with complete justice, that he had forgotten half of what she had taught him overnight. His left wrist kept collapsing. His fingers kept stiffening at the wrong moment. Every time it happened he had to start again.

Behind him, the camp was waking up in pieces. Petra had the fire going, crouched low over a blackened pot with her bandaged hand wrapped around the spoon because the cold still made the knuckles seize. Her bad left hip pulled her sideways whenever she shifted her weight, but she moved around it with the old mean patience of someone who had stopped expecting the body to cooperate years ago. Errin Denna had four clay jars open on the table by the shelter entrance and her nose nearly in one of them, making the same small dissatisfied sound every time the overnight seep had turned a sample. Kael sat on an upturned bucket near the eastern lean-to, chewing root and watching Maren's hands with the expression of a man trying not to laugh at somebody else's bad morning. Ruald worked at the water barrel, rewrapping the grey-brown cloth strips he used for his own half-improvised containment work. The strips smelled faintly of burnt sugar and old residue. He had mentioned once, while complaining about a boot seam, that his wife folded them for him at home, matching the corners exactly because she hated crooked cloth.

Denna took the little bone awl from behind her ear, scratched a fresh notch into the leather tally tied to her belt, and put the awl back without looking. She ate with her left hand and wrote with her right. That was one of the only things Maren knew about her, along with the fact that Thessa called her Denna and Kael called her Errin when he forgot himself. Hale was farther south on first-light watch where the valley rose toward the old residue beds. From here he was mostly a dark shape against the grey, standing still in the way only watchful men stood still, like movement was something to be spent carefully.

"No shoulder," Thessa said behind him.

Maren flinched hard enough that the bowl slipped against his thigh. He turned and found her already up, hair flattened on one side from sleep, coat only half buttoned. "You people move too quietly for honest work."

"You open from the shoulder again, you'll tear the pull crooked and contaminate your own lungs." She crouched without ceremony, caught his left wrist between two dry fingers, and turned it a few degrees inward. "There. Now do it the way I showed you."

"You say good morning like a threat."

"If you wanted pleasant company, you should not have come to a work camp."

Petra snorted over the pot. Kael spat root into the dirt and nudged his bucket a little with his heel so he could get a better view. Maren tried the motion again. His wrist held this time. His fingers did not.

"Too stiff," Thessa said.

"I know."

"Then stop doing it wrong."

Denna lifted one of the jars, frowned at it, and said, "This one's gone bitter."

"Bury it deeper," Thessa said without turning. "And stop breathing into the mouth."

"I'm not breathing into the mouth."

"Then your face is too close to it for some other stupid reason."

Ruald laughed once through his nose. Petra said, "You can tell she's rested."

It was such a normal camp line that for one stupid second Maren let himself believe in a normal morning. The pot hissed. Kael worked the root from one cheek to the other. Denna set the bad jar aside and reached for another. Hale stayed motionless on the ridge. Thessa released Maren's wrist.

Ruald came over from the barrel with one strip half wrapped around his hand. "You're closing too soon," he said to Maren. "Looks like you're trying to strangle it."

"Very helpful."

"You want helpful, stop doing that."

Denna held out a stopper without looking. "If you've got spare opinions, cork this."

Ruald thumbed the stopper into the mouth of the jar and handed it back. Denna twisted it tight, scratched another notch into the tally, and muttered, "Fourth bad turn this week."

Petra tasted the spoon, frowned, and shook another pinch of dried herb into the pot. "Everything's turning early."

"Not everything," Kael said around the root. He nudged a flat spare stone a little closer to the fire with his heel. "Maren's still useless right on schedule."

"Comforting."

"You'll know if I'm trying to comfort you."

Petra set out six tin cups by feel, one of them mashed into an oval from some older accident. On the ridge Hale lifted one hand without looking back, the signal that he was still there and had not seen anything worth shouting about. Denna tucked the awl behind her ear again. Ruald went back to his strips. The valley hummed under all of it like a second weather.

*Four hostiles approaching from the northwest ridge.*

Aldric's voice came fast and flat. Under that clipped delivery sat something worse than urgency. Recognition.

*They are in full combat loadout. Caul is leading them. Two melee fighters and one support caster.*

Maren came up off his knees so fast his ankle folded under him. He grabbed the bowl by its wrapping on reflex and caught himself against the trunk of a dead birch. "How far?"

*Quarter mile. Descending now. The caster is already building a targeting overlay. They have the camp.*

The bowl knocked against his wrist. He had known Caul would come. Derry had given him the direction and Caul was a tracker, patient in the way men got patient when patience was the only thing between them and the grief underneath. Maren had still thought he would have longer than this. A day, maybe two. Enough time to turn instruction into something less embarrassing than whatever he was currently doing in the dirt.

He ran.

"Heroes," he said as he crossed the camp. "Four. Northwest ridge. They've got a targeting overlay on us."

Petra's spoon stopped halfway to the pot rim. Denna's hand closed around the neck of the nearest jar. Kael's head snapped toward the ridge and the root stayed in his cheek. Ruald did not look up from the cloth at first, which was how frightened he was.

Thessa came out of the largest lean-to already awake enough to be angry. Her hair was still flattened on one side and both hands were empty. That meant she had put something down the second she heard his feet.

"How many?"

"Four. Ranger, two melee, one caster. The ranger's Caul."

The recalculation happened in her face all at once. "Leave the shelters. Carry only what matters. Denna, take the jars. Petra, leave the pot. Ruald, bring your strips. Kael, call Hale in. South to the third mouth."

"We can't outrun a hero party," Ruald said. The cloth strips hung from one hand, limp and dirty. "Thessa, we're not combat-classified. We don't have any way to"

"I'm not asking you to fight. The contamination around the third mouth is deep enough to blur target locks." She looked at Maren then, and that look had no warmth in it at all. "You're why they're here."

"Yeah."

"Then you stay and buy us the time."

It was not a question. Thessa turned and started moving the camp with the clipped, hard precision she used when work had gone bad enough that discussion was wasted breath. Denna swept the jars into a padded sling with the tally thumping against her thigh. Petra shoved the spoon aside hard enough to knock broth into the fire. Kael was already shouting for Hale down the slope. Ruald bent once for his satchel, missed the buckle, swore under his breath, and tried again.

*They will not make clean distance,* Vess said. *The targeting overlay covers the full valley.*

*Confirmed. The sin-eaters are flagged as non-combatant civilians.* Aldric paused. *Collateral authorization has been granted. The bounty's threat threshold overrides civilian protection.*

"What does that mean in actual words?"

*It means they are allowed to kill anyone between them and you.*

The first arrow hit the eastern lean-to's support pole with a flat crack and the whole side of the shelter sagged at once. Maren never heard the bow. He heard the impact. He heard Petra suck in a breath. Then a second arrow buried itself in the dirt three feet from Ruald's boots.

"Move," Maren shouted. "Ruald, move, don't stand there, move."

The two melee heroes came over the north lip of the camp at a run. One tall with a polearm. One shorter, fast, dual blades out. Maren did not have the data for them, only the shapes and the speed and the way his own body recognized trouble before his mind could sort it. Aldric's training surged into his muscles. Vess's killing instinct came up cold along his spine. By the time he decided to put himself between the fighters and the retreating sin-eaters, his feet had already done it.

The tall one reached him first. He felt the air move before he properly understood the sweep. Low strike for the legs. His body had already stepped out of it, half turned, hand clamped to the shaft to shove the arc past his hip. The weapon's weight jarred up through his wrists. Leather oil. Road sweat. Something sharp and herbal in the woman's armor padding.

*Third-tier melee. Polearm specialist. Inside her reach and the advantage disappears.*

He was already inside it. His elbow crashed into the side of her head above the ear guard. She staggered.

The second fighter came from the right with dual blades out, young enough that his face still had some softness left in it. Maren only caught that much before his body took over again. Forearm against the flat of one blade. Pivot outside the other. Knee into the boy's stance hard enough to make it buckle.

He was fighting and not fighting. The fragments operated his body while Maren occupied it, dragged through the thing by sensation and weight and smell. No damage numbers. No threat markers. Just the shock of impact in his bones, contaminated dirt giving under his boots, metal scraping leather.

Petra screamed, high and ragged and stripped of anything that sounded like fight. Maren's head turned while the rest of him was still engaged with the boy. Petra was down by the fire pit, black broth and ash running together around her knees. The arrow was lodged below her left shoulder blade. She had made it maybe three dragging body-lengths before the bad hip folded under her. Her bandaged hand clawed into the dirt hard enough to turn the knuckles white through old residue.

*Caul. Northwest ridge. Sixty yards and cycling another shot.*

The second arrow took Ruald in the throat.

He had turned back when Petra went down. That was the whole stupid human thing of it. He had turned back for a woman he had worked beside for years in a job nobody respected, and the broadhead found the gap under his jaw. He sat down hard as if a stool had been yanked out from under him. His hands went to his neck. The cloth strips spilled from the satchel, grey-brown in the dirt, still folded square from somebody else's careful hands.

*Left,* Vess said. *The polearm fighter is up again.*

His body spun. The overhead chop came down. Maren ducked under it and drove his fist into the woman's diaphragm. She folded. Something in her knee gave with a branch-snap sound and she went to the ground clutching it.

A dry pop went off east of the camp, not loud, just wrong, like a board cracking inside a closed room.

Denna jerked in the open ground between the second lean-to and the path south. One jar burst against a stone and sprayed dark slurry over her skirt. She dropped to both knees with the second jar still hugged to her ribs, stared at nothing for half a heartbeat, and folded onto her side. The leather tally knocked twice against her thigh and went still. Her right hand never let go of the bone awl. It drove point-first into the dirt beside her cheek and pinned two strands of her hair under it.

Kael slid the last few feet to her and caught her shoulder too late. "Denna's down," he said, and his voice had gone so flat it barely sounded like him. "The caster hit her with some kind of pulse. She's not moving."

Maren had enough time to count them and not enough time to reach any of them. Petra dragging blood and ash. Ruald drowning on his own throat. Denna on her side with the broken jar stinking in the dirt and the awl planted by her mouth like somebody had marked the place where she stopped. Three people down because Caul had followed him here.

*The caster is repositioning east,* Aldric said. *She is trying to establish a precision lock on you.*

Maren looked south. Thessa was fifty yards out, moving fast. Kael had Denna's shoulder once in his hand and then let it go because there was nothing there for him to do. Hale was higher on the slope, waving them toward the older contamination and then turning back long enough to make sure they kept moving. Behind Maren, the polearm woman was on the ground with her ruined knee, the boy was retreating uphill toward Caul, and the caster was somewhere he could not see because he could not see anything the System built.

*Maren.* Aldric's voice had lost its report-cadence. *The caster's next pulse will establish a range lock. If she completes the scan, they will be able to strike through the contamination.*

"How long?"

*Thirty seconds. Perhaps less.*

Maren looked past the practice patch near his knees to the residue pool between the first and second dungeon mouths, a shallow lowland full of years of accumulated contamination, cold-glowing and dense enough that even he could read its shape. The smell coming off it was layered and heavy, fresh ozone over old sweetness over the sour metal stink of residue that had been sitting in the same dirt long enough to change the dirt.

*No,* Aldric said at once. *That is a nexus-grade pool. You consumed a fraction of a minor pool under supervision and it nearly broke you.*

"If I don't do it, the caster gets the lock and everybody on that slope dies."

*You cannot control that much contamination.*

"I'm not trying to control it."

He was already running. The bowl thumped against his side. His boots slid on the bad ground. He hurdled one of Denna's broken jar shards and nearly went down anyway. Inside him the entity pressed outward as if it could feel what he was about to do and had reached for it before he had.

Vess did not answer. Her silence was its own verdict.

---

He dropped at the edge of the pool and yanked the wrapped bowl free because it was in the way. He let it fall beside him in the dirt. The contamination hummed through his trousers and into his shins, not the background hum of the valley but a local vibration with its own pitch. The glow was cold. He could feel it on the skin of his hands like the pressure of someone staring.

He used the second opening, the one Thessa had called reckless and emergency-only and too easy to do wrong. The opening did not feel like skill. It felt like stopping the body's natural flinch. Like unlatching something in himself that every sane person kept shut.

The contamination came in like floodwater, nothing like yesterday's supervised pull. It was cold and massive, more pressure than his body had any language for. His chest filled with something that was not air and was not liquid but had the weight of both. Ozone coated the back of his tongue. Copper. A sweetness on the turn toward rot. His teeth ached all the way up into the roots. The bones behind his eyes felt squeezed inward by hands that did not exist.

*The targeting overlay is degrading,* Aldric said, voice gone distant. *Their interface is flickering around the consumption point.*

More. He pulled more. The contamination rushed at the opening as if it had been waiting for one. That was the part nobody had explained to him. It wanted to go somewhere. It poured toward him with a horrible eagerness and the pool's glow dimmed as the residue went into him.

Inside his skull the unclassified thing responded at once.

It surged.

His bones started vibrating at a frequency that did not belong to any human skeleton. Every muscle in his back clenched at once, not like a cramp but like something had reached into him and tried to close a fist. His knee slammed down on the bowl where he had dropped it. Ceramic cracked sharp under him and a shard punched through his trouser leg into the skin above his kneecap. The pain barely registered.

*Maren, stop,* Vess said. For the first time since he had known her, the flatness in her voice had split. *The entity is expanding beyond your containment threshold.*

He could not stop. The consumption had momentum now. The pool was feeding into him faster than he could regulate and the entity was drinking from the same channel, unfolding inside his head, pressing against his consciousness like something trying to stand in a room that had been built too low for it.

*The System sees it.* Aldric sounded nothing like himself. *It is not registering a threat class. It is registering a void, a gap in the architecture.*

The pool went dark without going empty, as if the light had been eaten out of it and left the residue behind. The last of the contamination slammed into him, the channel snapped shut, and Maren was on his hands and knees over dead soil retching uselessly because it was not in his stomach. It was everywhere else.

The hum from the dungeon mouths jumped an octave in a single hard step, going from the low furnace-bass he had been hearing for two days to something high enough to scrape his sinuses raw. The air temperature dropped with it. One breath and the early-autumn morning was gone, replaced by the damp cold of a cellar.

*Local reclassification in progress,* Aldric said, and now the old formal cadence was back because fear had driven him into it. *Crook Valley redesignation. Threat classification: Critical. Contamination index: Extreme. Authorized response: full containment protocol.*

"What does that mean?"

*It means the System is treating this valley as a breach site. Every entity inside the perimeter is now a valid threat target. Including Caul's party.*

---

The quest-light came up first. It burst through the soil in hard bright runners, cold threads racing over the discolored ground with the speed of roots finding water. In seconds the valley floor was webbed in it, pale branching lines connecting and reconnecting until the whole low ground looked netted in visible structure.

Then the contamination spiked. The smell changed before anything else did, sweetness turning acrid, the air going thick enough that every breath had weight. The nearest dungeon mouth began giving off shapes.

They came out wrong, with too many joints and limbs bending where living bodies did not. Dog-sized things lit from inside with the same cold light running through the valley floor, threat creatures the System had made for this exact correction, an immune response given bodies.

The first one oriented toward Maren, stopped, and turned its malformed head toward the northwest ridge instead.

*They are not prioritizing you,* Aldric said quietly. *The entity makes you unreadable. They are going for anything the System can classify.*

"Caul's people."

*Yes.*

From the ridge Caul shouted, voice gone hoarse enough that Maren almost did not recognize it. "Fall back to the ridge. All units, disengage the target. We have a local correction." He broke off. Even at this distance there was still that briefing-cadence in him, trained and practiced and trying to hold. "No. Read that again. Friendly assets inside breach perimeter..." Something struck something else with a sound that was not flesh and was not stone. "Acceptable loss? We are the response team."

*He is reading the containment directive,* Aldric said. *It has marked his own party expendable until classification stability is restored.*

The polearm woman screamed.

*She's down,* Vess said. *The creatures reached her before she cleared the zone.*

Maren got to his feet. Everything about his body felt wrong now, too heavy and badly balanced, as if the contamination inside him had been packed unevenly through his bones. The cracked bowl lay in the dirt at his feet with the cloth half caught beneath it. He snatched it up by reflex.

More creatures were coming out of all three mouths. The valley floor started to crawl.

The young dual-blade fighter was crying now, wet and ragged between the clang of his blades against bodies the System itself had generated. Caul said, "No." Then again, louder and in a voice that had stopped sounding like a report. "No. Ignore the directive. Forget the target. Get behind me."

He turned his whole body away from Maren.

Whatever pursuit had brought him here was over. Caul planted himself between the creatures and the boy on the ridge, dropped any pretense that the hunt still mattered, and started cutting down what the System had sent at his own people.

Thessa appeared at Maren's side carrying one of Denna's clay jars under her arm. He had not heard her approach. She moved through contaminated ground the way other people moved through their own kitchens, feet finding the safe patches without hesitation. A smear of dark slurry had dried along the jar's shoulder where it had slopped during the run.

"Three jars left," she said. "That buys us minutes, not many."

"Who got out?"

"Kael and Hale are over the south ridge already." Her tone did not shift. "Petra didn't get back up. Ruald bled where he fell. Denna took the pulse clean and never came round."

There was no room to answer that. Thessa was moving already, and Maren followed because the valley floor had started to move in ways the human body could not survive for long.

They cut south past what had been the camp. Petra had made it almost to the path. She was on her face now, arrow still in her back, one boot twisted under the ruined line of her hip. The bandaged hand that had held the spoon all morning was buried to the knuckles in the dirt as if she had tried to drag the rest of herself forward by that one grip alone. Ruald sat where Caul's arrow had put him down, chin on his chest, the blood down the front of him already drying dark. One of the cloth strips had wound itself around his wrist in the fall, still folded square, neat as a prayer somebody else had said for him. Denna lay farther south on her side with the unbroken jar missing from the crook of her arm and the awl still planted in the dirt by her cheek. The tally at her belt had swung under her and pinned itself there. Thessa did not look directly at any of them. She bent once, snatched a strip from Ruald's spill with her free hand, and kept walking.

The thing that came at them from the nearest line of quest-light moved low and fast. Thessa uncapped Denna's jar one-handed, flung a thick ribbon of old residue across the ground in front of it, and the creature hit the slick patch with all its weight. Its forelegs shot sideways. It went over hard on too many elbows, skidding broadside into a deeper puddle where the joints began to dissolve from the inside. Thessa did not even watch it finish.

"Step where I step," she said. "Not near it. On it."

Maren tried. The consumed residue made his legs thick and unreliable, every step a negotiation between his own body and the thing packed inside it. The cracked bowl knocked his hip. The cut above his kneecap had started a hot wet trickle down his shin. Twice he planted a boot and the ground seemed to lean away under him.

Ahead, quest-light webbed the route south in bright threads no wider than a finger. Thessa moved through them without hesitation, choosing the gaps with a speed that looked like instinct and was probably three decades of not dying. Maren misjudged one line and his boot came down an inch too close.

The light snapped upward, not touching him, but close enough to throw cold across his skin.

Thessa caught the back of his coat with Ruald's cloth strip and yanked him sideways so hard his shoulder popped. "I said step where I step."

"I'm trying."

"Try with your feet."

Another creature turned toward them, head canting as the light shivered around Maren's near miss. Thessa slashed the cloth strip down across the bright thread. Old contamination soaked through the weave, turning the line muddy and thick for one blink. The creature swerved after the false signal and vanished into a knot of brighter runners ten yards east. The knot erupted. Cold light went up in a spray and the thing came apart in pieces that did not look anatomical anymore.

Thessa looped the dirty strip around her wrist and kept going. That one quick salvage from Ruald was part of the route now, part of the work. Maren hated her for the competence of it and would have hated her more if she had done anything else.

Behind them Caul shouted again, closer now because the ridge carried sound. "Stay with me. Leave the target. That's an order." There was a metallic crash. Then, lower and rougher, a line Maren almost missed under the valley hum. "The directive's wrong."

*Caul has abandoned pursuit,* Vess said. *He is holding the ridge for the surviving melee fighter. The caster is down.*

Maren stopped at the foot of the south slope. Below him the valley seethed with light. On the far side of the camp the support caster was sprawled half off the eastern bank, one arm still flung outward in the posture that had dropped Denna. Caul held the higher ground with the dual-blade boy behind him. He was not looking toward Maren at all anymore. He was looking at what the System had decided to spend.

*He has a route out,* Aldric said softly. *The perimeter is open on the northwest side. He could leave if he chose to.*

"He won't leave the boy."

*No,* Aldric said. *He will not.*

Thessa turned and looked back at him from ten yards up the slope. There was clay on her sleeve from Denna's jar and Ruald's strip hanging from her wrist. "Move. There's nothing you can do for them from here."

She meant Caul's people. She meant the ones in the camp below too. She meant all of it. Maren hated that she was right, but that did not make her less right. He climbed.

The south slope was steep and slick with old residue worked so deep into the soil it had stopped feeling accidental. The smell changed as they rose, less raw ozone, more earth gone strange over time. Twice Maren slipped and once Thessa caught him by the elbow without slowing. The jar under her arm knocked against her ribs with each step. She never set it down.

They crested the ridge. The far side dropped into scrub and thin trees already starting to turn for autumn. The air out there was cleaner by degrees, which was enough to feel like a different world.

Maren turned once before following her down. From the ridge he could see the whole valley. The camp was gone inside the web of quest-light. The fire pit was just another dark knot inside the pale grid. On the far side Caul still held the higher ground with the boy crouched behind him. He had backed almost to the opening Aldric had described, one shoulder turned to cover the retreat, every cut of the sword buying somebody else another step.

*The boy is alive,* Aldric said. *The response is already narrowing toward the center of the breach.*

Three of them, Maren thought. The sentence would not stay thought-sized. It came out of his mouth. "Three of them. Because of me."

Thessa stood beside him long enough to answer. "Yes."

She started down the far slope. Maren followed. The cracked bowl knocked against his hip through the cloth at every step, and behind him the valley kept humming.
