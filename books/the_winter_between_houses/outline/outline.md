# The Winter Between Houses

## Primary Narrative Engine (determines chapter consequences)
**Combination engine:** literary fiction + character study + domestic love story.

This is not a thriller or mystery engine. The storm is a pressure system, not the plot's villain. Chapters are driven primarily by relational leverage, private shame, bodily vulnerability, class economics, and shifting self-knowledge inside two marriages. Consequences are mostly emotional, relational, informational, and material (housing, money, medical access), with physical jeopardy arriving through weather and pregnancy rather than crime escalation.

## Consequence Logic
- Every chapter changes a relationship state, a knowledge state, or a material survival state.
- Intimacy is treated as event-level causality, not background implication.
- Quiet chapters establish human baseline (meal, chores, non-strategic talk), so crisis chapters land with force.
- At least five chapters use non-confrontational engines: slow-burn discovery, preparation/dread, acted-upon pressure, aftermath/inventory, and information turns.

## Chapter Plan (18 Chapters)

### 1) First Snow on Barrow Lane
- **POV/texture:** Elsie close-third; 3 scenes; observational setup with pockets of tense dialogue.
- **Objective:** Keep domestic normalcy as the first severe snow hits.
- **Conflict:** Tom has spent heating money; Elsie must ask neighbor Noreen for coal.
- **Consequence:** Material dependence begins between the two women; Elsie's marriage facade cracks.
- **Causal handoff:** Debt anxiety drives Elsie into concealment in Ch. 2.

### 2) The Envelope with Red Print
- **POV/texture:** Noreen close-third; 2 scenes; information-driven, low action.
- **Objective:** Return a misdelivered letter without starting street gossip.
- **Conflict:** The letter reveals arrears on Elsie's baby pram and forged authorization.
- **Consequence:** Noreen and Elsie become bound by a shared financial secret.
- **Causal handoff:** Complicity raises stakes for the Christmas gathering in Ch. 3.

### 3) Christmas Table, Missing Plates
- **POV/texture:** Split Elsie/Noreen; 4 scenes; ensemble meal chapter with humor and social cuts.
- **Objective:** Stage a normal holiday meal across both households.
- **Conflict:** Scarcity, class shame, and Peter's public condescension destabilize everyone.
- **Consequence:** Noreen registers Peter's contempt as chronic, not incidental.
- **Causal handoff:** Emotional distance in the Walsh marriage primes Ch. 4–5 dependency.

### 4) Whiteout
- **POV/texture:** Elsie; 2 long scenes; dread chapter (preparation without climax).
- **Objective:** Locate Tom before roads close.
- **Conflict:** Transit shuts down; Tom doesn't return; phone lines fail.
- **Consequence:** Elsie spends the night in Noreen's house and discloses her prior stillbirth.
- **Causal handoff:** Shared vulnerability makes Ch. 5 medical emergency possible.

### 5) The Wrong Kind of Pain
- **POV/texture:** Noreen; 3 scenes; acted-upon chapter.
- **Objective:** Get help for sudden bleeding and contractions.
- **Conflict:** Peter dismisses symptoms; roads are impassable; hospital unreachable.
- **Consequence:** False labor passes, but Noreen's trust in Peter's judgment fractures.
- **Causal handoff:** Survival labor shifts to women-led logistics in Ch. 6.

### 6) Coal Arithmetic
- **POV/texture:** Elsie; 2 scenes; inventory/aftermath rhythm.
- **Objective:** Stretch food, coal, and paraffin through an uncertain freeze.
- **Conflict:** Husbands claim decision power while women do the ration math.
- **Consequence:** Elsie secretly moves supplies to Noreen; Tom reads this as betrayal.
- **Causal handoff:** Household surveillance intensifies in Ch. 7.

### 7) A Man in the Spare Room
- **POV/texture:** Noreen; 3 scenes; social pressure chapter.
- **Objective:** Preserve privacy after council billets a stranded linesman, Arthur Hale.
- **Conflict:** A witness now sees each marriage at close range.
- **Consequence:** Public-deniable abuse/private reality gap narrows; shame becomes external.
- **Causal handoff:** Arthur's presence indirectly enables discovery in Ch. 8.

### 8) Ledger Marks
- **POV/texture:** Noreen to Elsie handoff; 2 scenes; slow-burn discovery.
- **Objective:** Return kitchenware and keep boundaries intact.
- **Conflict:** Noreen finds bookmaker IOUs and evidence Tom forged Elsie's name.
- **Consequence:** Elsie learns the debt can become legal jeopardy, not just marital deceit.
- **Causal handoff:** Fear and tenderness collapse into Ch. 9 intimacy.

### 9) Heatless Night
- **POV/texture:** Alternating Elsie/Noreen; 3 scenes; intimate high-intensity chapter.
- **Objective:** Endure a full-night blackout safely.
- **Conflict:** Desire collides with vows, pregnancy fear, and moral self-image.
- **Consequence:** They become physically intimate; the relationship state changes irreversibly.
- **Causal handoff:** Ch. 10 deals with behavior after an irreversible act.

### 10) Morning Inventory
- **POV/texture:** Elsie; 2 scenes; quiet aftermath with domestic detail.
- **Objective:** Decide terms of contact after the night together.
- **Conflict:** Shame, longing, and practical interdependence pull in opposite directions.
- **Consequence:** They choose secrecy plus continued alliance, creating chronic coded tension.
- **Causal handoff:** Suppressed strain distorts responses in Ch. 11.

### 11) Fall on the Ice
- **POV/texture:** Noreen; 3 scenes; injury chapter.
- **Objective:** Secure treatment after Peter fractures his wrist in a drunken fall.
- **Conflict:** Peter's pain sharpens control tactics; Noreen loses time for prenatal care.
- **Consequence:** Missed clinic review increases medical danger and dependence.
- **Causal handoff:** Ch. 12 delivers the medical information turn.

### 12) Clinic Thursday
- **POV/texture:** Noreen; 2 scenes; information-turn chapter.
- **Objective:** Get professional guidance despite Peter's objections.
- **Conflict:** Midwife Keane diagnoses escalating preeclampsia risk and strict rest requirements impossible at home.
- **Consequence:** Pregnancy risk becomes immediate and concrete; secrecy now carries bodily stakes.
- **Causal handoff:** Decisions in Ch. 13 are made under mortality pressure.

### 13) Ring
- **POV/texture:** Elsie; 3 scenes; confrontation chapter.
- **Objective:** Prevent criminal charges linked to Tom's depot theft.
- **Conflict:** Tom demands Elsie pawn her ring and lie to police.
- **Consequence:** Elsie refuses and Tom leaves for his brother's, collapsing the marriage's financial structure.
- **Causal handoff:** Both households are exposed when labor begins in Ch. 14.

### 14) House Birth
- **POV/texture:** Multi-POV tight focus; 4 scenes; major external pressure chapter.
- **Objective:** Keep Noreen and premature infant alive during a whiteout.
- **Conflict:** Ambulance cannot reach Barrow Lane; hemorrhage risk escalates; heat is failing.
- **Consequence:** Baby is born alive but fragile; Noreen survives severe blood loss and cannot return to old domestic terms.
- **Causal handoff:** Ch. 15 tracks the emotional and institutional aftermath.

### 15) Milk Train
- **POV/texture:** Noreen; 2 scenes; aftermath/inventory chapter.
- **Objective:** Re-enter ordinary time after emergency birth.
- **Conflict:** Hospital transfer rules separate mother and infant; Peter recasts crisis as debt she owes him.
- **Consequence:** Noreen emotionally exits the marriage before physically leaving it.
- **Causal handoff:** Ch. 16 moves from interior exit to logistical exit.

### 16) Saint Brigid House
- **POV/texture:** Noreen with Elsie support; 3 scenes; acted-upon then decisive movement.
- **Objective:** Reach safe lodging before Peter can reassert legal/domestic control.
- **Conflict:** Peter finds evidence of Noreen and Elsie's intimacy and threatens custody tactics.
- **Consequence:** Noreen leaves for a church-run mothers' hostel with Keane's help; separation becomes public fact.
- **Causal handoff:** Elsie must face birth and naming decisions in Ch. 17.

### 17) Water Under the Doors
- **POV/texture:** Elsie; 3 scenes; labor chapter with relational reversal.
- **Objective:** Deliver Elsie's baby during thaw flooding with Tom absent.
- **Conflict:** Labor accelerates; travel blocked; social expectation says husband should lead.
- **Consequence:** Elsie names Noreen emergency guardian, publicly reordering kinship.
- **Causal handoff:** Ch. 18 resolves long-term terms, not fairy-tale closure.

### 18) Thaw
- **POV/texture:** Alternating Elsie/Noreen; 2 scenes; reflective coda with practical stakes.
- **Objective:** Set spring survival terms under debt, gossip, and infant uncertainty.
- **Conflict:** Law, church pressure, and economics resist both women's chosen futures.
- **Consequence:** Neither marriage returns to old form; both women choose costly autonomy and sustained neighbor-kin bond.
- **Causal handoff:** End state supports sequel-free closure with open emotional weather.
