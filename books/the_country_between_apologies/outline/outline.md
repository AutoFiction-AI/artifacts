# Narrative Engine Determination
Primary engine: **literary fiction + character study + late-life coming-of-age**, with a secondary relational engine (marital fracture and brief extramarital attachment).

Why this engine fits the premise:
- The premise centers on a man fleeing ordinary obligations, so the strongest consequences are emotional, relational, economic, and perceptual rather than criminal or tactical.
- Chapter turns are driven by shame, avoidance, financial fallout, and changing self-understanding.
- External events (weather, injury, debt enforcement) function as pressure on character, not genre-thriller escalation.

Consequence model used for chapter design:
- Relational: trust breaks, boundaries set, limited reconnection earned.
- Economic/institutional: job abandonment, lost insurance, debt deadlines, wage exploitation.
- Psychological: self-myth of "provider/competent father" collapses, replaced by accountable but diminished adulthood.
- Informational: voicemail/legal/tuition facts trigger major turns without direct confrontation.

# Causal Spine
1. Eli drops June at college and cannot tolerate the sudden vacancy of home.
2. He keeps driving west, then destroys normal contact channels.
3. Mechanical failure forces him into temporary labor, where his flight becomes a spoken fact.
4. Nora discovers missing emergency funds; his escape now causes concrete harm.
5. He seeks refuge with estranged son Theo and is forced to face prior parental failures.
6. While hiding there, he loses his job and health insurance.
7. He leaves with promises he cannot yet keep, trying to outrun shame with motion.
8. He liquidates symbols of marriage to continue west.
9. A whiteout crash removes his last illusion of control.
10. In recovery, he builds a fragile interim life with motel work.
11. Intimacy with Marisol gives real tenderness and real betrayal.
12. A factual voicemail (legal filing, tuition cuts, frozen accounts) changes his direction.
13. Debt structures act on him; he is stranded without vehicle power.
14. He enters exploitative labor to generate repayment cash.
15. A coworker overdose breaks his avoidance; he asks Theo for logistical help.
16. He returns east, ends the affair honestly, and transfers money before arriving.
17. Home reentry happens under basement terms and legal boundaries, not forgiveness.
18. He tells June the full truth; she sets limits but restores minimal contact, and he chooses sustained responsibility over disappearance.

# Texture Variance Check
- Quiet baseline chapters: 6, 11, 16 (plus portions of 3 and 17).
- Non debate-then-action engines explicitly used: 3 (slow-burn discovery), 8 (preparation/dread), 9 and 13 (acted upon), 10 (aftermath/inventory), 12 (information turn).
- Stakes progression: emotional dislocation -> financial/institutional harm -> bodily vulnerability -> limited relational repair.
