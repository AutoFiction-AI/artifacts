Everyone is born with a full Ledger — max level, every ability unlocked. Power is preserved through inaction. The aristocracy of the Hoarded has ruled for centuries by never spending a single point, because every action permanently drains your stats and skills. The rational choice is to never act.

Vel Ashwick spent forty years actually using her abilities — fighting, healing, exploring, saving people the Hoarded abandoned. She's level 31 in a world where the average person dies at 280. She's also the only person who knows that level zero is a threshold to something beneath the Ledger: a world where people who've been spent down across the centuries have built a civilization without stats or skills. She needs to get there. She won't say why.

Kem Aldraste is Hoarded aristocracy, level 300, and has never used a single ability in his life. He has his own reasons for wanting to reach zero.

They're burning down together — her with surgical precision, him with reckless extravagance — while the Hoarded send max-level agents who've never thrown a punch to stop them. Every chapter they get weaker. Every chapter the enemies get closer. Every remaining ability is a choice: spend it now to survive, or save it and suffer through on pure experience and guts.

Book 1 of the Spent saga. Action, dark comedy, and the question of what power is for if not to use it.

Tone: action-driven LitRPG, dark comedy with genuine emotional stakes.
