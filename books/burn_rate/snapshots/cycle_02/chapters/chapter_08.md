# Chapter 8

Vel counted the road south in levels.

The Spent Towns fell behind them over three days. South of the last managed farms, the trade road narrowed to dirt and the fields went from rich to merely serviceable. Spring had settled into warmth. The air smelled like cut grain and drying mud.

Twenty-eight. That number sat behind everything else.

Dren walked point. He read the track by habit: fresh ruts, local bootprints, last night's soft spots. Twenty years on the southern routes showed in how little of his attention looked busy.

"There's a depot settlement about an hour ahead," he said. "Grain storage, maybe an inn. We fill water there and keep going."

"How big?" Vel asked.

"Thirty, forty. Farms around here feed into it."

Kem walked behind her with his own pack and, for once, didn't fight the straps. He kept looking at the fields as if open land were still something he had to learn in pieces.

"Is that wheat?" he asked.

"Barley," Dren said.

"Right."

"You can tell by the awns."

"I know. Vel told me."

"Hm." Dren glanced at the field. "Charm's running thin on this stretch. Means the depot folk will be touchy about strangers near the silo."

The road dust settled on Vel's boots. Somewhere east, a Shaping strike cracked against stone. Ordinary sound. Useful sound. She was listening past it.

What she expected from pursuit was precision. The Hoarded liked the cheapest clean answer. A Perception pulse. A tracker. A small team that knew how to spend one level exactly where it mattered.

What came out of the trees crashed through brush, snagged cloth, and hit the road already losing to the undergrowth.

"Off the road," Vel said.

Dren moved at once. Kem caught his pack on a fencepost and the wire sang.

Too slow. Too loud. Too late.

Five figures broke from the treeline in matching gray cloaks. Three wore them wrong. One had cinched his hood until his face was a sweating red circle. One had cut armholes into his and turned it into a vest. One had lost hers somewhere in the woods. The fifth had his cloak on properly and looked unhappy about it.

Level 300. All five.

Their Ledgers pressed against the air. Five maxed bodies, enough stored potential to make the background hum feel heavy.

Vel calculated. Five at 300, untrained. Herself at 28. Kem at about 225 and still unable to measure himself. Dren at 138, not built for a fight. The grain depot sat ahead with a stone silo and a few people moving between buildings. Civilians in range. No cover worth the name.

If Kem threw a perimeter now, he would throw it the size of a meeting hall. They would be stuck inside a shining dome in the middle of flat ground while five level-300 idiots circled it and pounded it, and he would burn half his useful margin before they reached the trees. She needed movement. She needed the Hoarded to stay stupid for one more minute.

"Kem. Behind me. Don't cast unless I tell you."

"I can fight."

"Not yet. You'll pen us in."

The hooded agent tried a Binding first. His hands moved with meticulous care. The technique was textbook and dead wrong in the body.

At 300, a proper Binding could have pinned all three of them. He missed everyone and hit a boulder.

His palm sealed to the rock with a wet slap. He jerked sideways and ended up half hanging from the stone, boots skidding in the dirt.

"That's not right," he said. "My hand is. I can't get my hand off."

The bare-armed woman opened a Perception Sweep. Vel knew the posture. At 300 it should have mapped targets, exits, structure.

Instead it gave her everything.

Every insect in the barley. Every crack in the silo mortar. Pollen in the air. Seventeen breathing people in the depot. Worms in the wet ground below the ditch.

The woman made a small ruined sound and sat down hard.

"Too much," she said. "There are seventeen. The silo wall is split on the south side. I can hear the worms. Too much."

Two useless already. Vel started angling south. If she could get Kem and Dren off the road now, the perimeter never had to happen.

The agent in the vest threw a Combat Pulse at Kem.

He missed by forty meters and hit the silo.

The upper third sheared off and became weather: blocks, dust, a roar of grain pouring into open air. Barley burst across the road in a yellow cloud that smelled warm and dry.

Stone hit Dren.

He had been crouched in the grass six meters from the road. A chunk of mortared stone caught his left forearm and opened it from near the elbow to midway down the wrist. The impact made a flat wet sound. Dren said "Shit" in the same flat tone he used for spoiled cargo, then looked down and saw the wound.

The edges gaped. Pink meat showed under the blood. Stone dust and grain chaff packed the cut.

He clamped his right hand over it. Blood pushed through his fingers at once.

Vel marked the wound and left it there. She could not stop for him yet.

The fourth agent did not aim at anyone. He wanted to try Strength.

He swung once at open air.

The blow shoved a ring of compressed air across the field. Barley flattened. Dust went sideways. Two trees snapped at the middle and dropped together.

The pressure hit Vel in the sternum from fifteen meters away. Preview only. Enough.

The last agent had been watching. Proper cloak. Proper stance. No rush. He had seen the failures and was sorting them.

His hands came up.

Force Push.

Vel turned. The edge of it hit her across the left ribs instead of the center of her chest.

Two cracks, then the hot wrong slide under them as bone flexed past sense and cartilage tore loose near the sternum. Breathing stopped making practical sense.

She hit the ground on her right shoulder, dragged in one shallow breath, and got up again.

Her hand clamped the fracture site before she told it to. Two ribs at least. Maybe three. The cartilage felt loose. Healing it would cost two levels. She was not spending two levels here.

Twenty-eight when the road started.

Vel burned a Combat strike into the proper-cloak agent's hip and knocked his stance sideways. His next Push went into the dirt.

One level. Twenty-seven.

The Strength agent wound up again. She spent an Evasion charm and stepped two feet right. The air where she'd been standing punched dust across her neck.

One level. Twenty-six.

Kem's Ledger shifted behind her. He had been waiting for the order since the moment the gray cloaks appeared. Now Dren was bleeding, the field was full of stone, and the one useful agent had learned to aim. The open-ground plan was dead.

"Kem. Perimeter. Now."

The barrier snapped up around them in a blue-white dome at least thirty meters wide. Too big by a factor of three. It crackled where barley dust struck it.

The next Push hit the shell and ran around it in a white ripple.

Vel drove a second Combat strike into the ground in front of the Strength agent. Dirt and stone burst into his face. He swung blind and tore a furrow through the field.

One level. Twenty-five.

A third strike took the proper-cloak agent in the knee. Not enough to damage him, enough to buckle him. The Push forming in his hands bled off into the barley.

One level. Twenty-four.

The hooded agent had torn himself free of the boulder by then. His palm was flayed raw. He threw another Binding. Vel spent a second Evasion charm and the thing took a fencepost instead.

One level. Twenty-three.

Her last Combat strike cost a turn of the torso that nearly put her back on the ground. Pain went white across her left side. She forced the motion anyway and hit the Strength agent in the shoulder. Something gave. His arm dropped. He went to one knee, mouth open, good hand clamped over the shoulder too late to matter.

One level. Twenty-two.

Six levels in less than three minutes. One idiot had skinned his palm on a boulder, another was kneeling in the barley with his shoulder hanging wrong, and the one in the proper cloak had still found her ribs.

Each breath ground bone against bone.

"We move," Vel said. Her voice came out quiet because her ribs would not let it come out any other way.

Dren was already upright, left arm clamped to his body.

"Tree cover," he said, jerking his chin south. "Half a kilometer."

"Can you run?"

He looked at his arm. Then at her side. "Can you?"

No. She could walk fast if she kept her torso rigid and her left arm locked against the break.

"Kem. Drop north. Reinforce south. Walk it."

The perimeter shifted. Kem walked backward with both hands out, face tight with concentration. The north side dissolved. The south wall thickened.

They moved. Dren first, off balance but fast. Vel after him, every step negotiated with her ribs. Kem came last, keeping the barrier between them and the field. A Binding hit it and spread. A half-formed Combat Pulse hit it and died there.

The agents did not pursue. The woman with the wrecked Perception was still on the ground. The hooded one stared at his palm. The agent in the vest stood by the ruined silo, staring from the broken stone to his own hand, baffled by both. The Strength agent knelt in the dirt. The proper-cloak agent watched them go.

He was the only one Vel watched back.

The trees closed overhead. Vel braced against an oak and checked the damage with her fingertips. Fifth and sixth rib on the left. Cartilage peeled loose near the sternum. A small Restoration would fix it for two levels and leave her at twenty. Twenty belonged to the Margins, not to this road. She pressed her palm over the break and left the bones where they were.

"Kem." He bent double, hands on his thighs, sweat running down the side of his face. "One-eighty-three."

Forty-two levels. A trained spender could have done that perimeter for half the cost, maybe less. Vel kept that to herself. The barrier had held. "Okay."

Dren eased himself down under a birch and made the wound bleed fresh when he checked it. Blood ran over his wrist and into the leaf litter. Vel found a clean strip of cloth in what was left of her pack and handed it over. "Hold it closed. Hard. There's water ahead?" He wrapped the cloth one-handed and pulled the knot tight with his teeth. "Creek crossing. Three kilometers." "That'll do." He flexed his fingers once, stopped, and said, "Some fucking professionals."

Through the trees, the field looked wrecked in pieces: five gray cloaks spread around the barley, settlement people keeping a careful distance, half a silo lying where it had landed. A sixth figure stood near the treeline apart from the others, not in gray, dark hair in a formal Hoarded arrangement coming loose, a notebook in one hand. She had not fought. She had watched.

The woman wrote while the others recovered. Vel could see where the pen paused: the broken silo, the perimeter's footprint in the flattened grain, the furrow cut by the Strength swing, the line they had taken into the woods. When she lifted her head, her gaze ran that route again, trunk to trunk, counting distance. Then she crossed the field back toward the gray cloaks at her own pace.

Next time they would know how wide Kem cast under pressure, how fast a bleeding man could make tree cover, and why Vel kept her left arm clamped to her ribs.

"We keep going," Vel said.

She pushed off the tree. Pain flared hard enough to blur the trunks. Kem watched her face and kept his mouth shut. Dren was already moving toward the creek.
