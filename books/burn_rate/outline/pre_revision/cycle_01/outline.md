# Novel Outline: Burn Rate

## Narrative Engine

**Primary engines:** Speculative fiction (LitRPG) + dark comedy + survival drama
**Secondary engines:** Character study, odd-couple partnership

The Ledger's inverted-power mechanic — where every action permanently weakens you — creates pressures unique to this speculative premise. Comedy arises from the absurdity of a civilization built on inaction: aristocrats who've never dressed themselves, max-level agents who've never thrown a punch, a social order where competence is a mark of low status. The survival drama escalates through the diminishing-returns mechanic: every chapter they get weaker, every ability used is gone forever, and the enemies have unlimited (if untrained) reserves.

State shifts are primarily physical + relational: the protagonists are measurably, numerically weaker at the end of every chapter, and their relationship to each other and to the concept of spending shifts alongside the numbers. Comic chapters should be fully comic — the dark comedy is a narrative mode, not seasoning on a drama.

## Point-of-View Structure

Third-person limited, past tense, rotating between **Vel Ashwick** and **Kem Aldraste** as primary POV characters, with two chapters from **Sable Orm** (the pursuer).

## Chapter Plan

### Chapter 1 — "What Thirty-One Looks Like"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Aftermath / preparation

Vel heals an injured farmer in a settlement the Hoarded have abandoned to disease. The healing costs her two levels she'd budgeted for the journey ahead. She checks her Ledger: 31. She's been planning the route to zero for years — mapping the path, working out the mechanism. She can't wait any longer; each job drains her closer to a level where she won't have enough left to make the crossing work. She's heard rumors of a Hoarded lord who walked out of the Vault. If he's real, and if he has the levels she needs, he solves her last remaining problem. She leaves the settlement heading north.

**State shift:** Vel commits to the journey. The countdown begins.

---

### Chapter 2 — "Lord of Nothing"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Comic escalation / departure

Kem Aldraste, level 300, sits through a Conservatory Rite — the Hoarded's weekly ritual of deliberate inaction, where great families demonstrate the discipline of doing nothing in proximity to objects that invite use. Kem watches his father, Lord Aldraste, who sits with perfect Hoarded composure. He's watched his father do this for twenty-six years. He's also watched his father's eyes go empty. The speech slowing, the personality thinning. The Hoarded call it Stillness and celebrate it. Kem sees it for what it is: his father is dying from the inside out, level 300 and rotting.

Kem stands up mid-rite. He uses his first ability — a Projection Shield, massive, crackling, absurdly overpowered for the purpose of opening a door. The room goes silent. His mother, Lady Aldraste, says his name once. He walks out.

Outside the Vault walls, Kem discovers: the world is louder than he thought, his legs are not used to walking more than a hundred meters, and he has no idea how anything works. His second ability use — a Sustenance Charm — produces enough food for forty people because he can't calibrate output. He's dropped twelve levels in an hour.

**State shift:** Kem breaks from the Hoarded. Free, incompetent, and dropping fast.

---

### Chapter 3 — "The Worst Possible Arrangement"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Confrontation / negotiation

Vel finds Kem three days into his freedom, already at 286, having spent levels on problems a child could solve without abilities. She's disgusted. He's everything wrong with the Hoarded — wasteful, ignorant, coddled. But he's exactly what she needs: someone with enough levels to fuel a sustained high-output cascade that the threshold mechanism requires. She needs him. She hates that she needs him.

They negotiate. Kem wants to know where zero leads. Vel says she'll tell him when he needs to know. Kem doesn't like this, but he has no better plan and no survival skills. Terms: she guides, he follows, they both reach zero. What she doesn't tell him: the mechanism requires something specific from a high-level spender, and she's been searching for a willing one for years.

**State shift:** Uneasy alliance formed. Vel has knowledge, Kem has levels, neither trusts the other.

---

### Chapter 4 — "What Three Hundred Feels Like"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Action / comic escalation

First real combat. Hoarded outriders — low-level enforcement agents at level 295 who've been permitted five levels of training — catch up. For Kem, this is the first time his abilities have ever been directed at a problem. It's revelatory and catastrophic. He throws a Combat Pulse meant to stun and levels a hillside. He activates a speed ability and runs into a tree because he's never moved that fast. He's impossibly powerful and has the tactical awareness of a toddler.

Vel handles the outriders with surgical efficiency — three abilities, three levels, three downed agents. Kem burns 41 levels accomplishing roughly the same result with a fraction of the precision. He's exhilarated. He's never felt his body work before. Vel wants to kill him — at this rate he'll be empty before they're a third of the way there.

**State shift:** Kem discovers the joy and cost of action. Pursuit is confirmed. Vel realizes his recklessness is a problem she cannot fully control.

---

### Chapter 5 — "Camp Manners"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Drift-under-pressure / tender

A quiet chapter. Two days south of the outrider fight, moving through forested highlands. Vel teaches Kem to do things without abilities: start a fire, cook meat, carry a pack without a Strength Charm. Kem is terrible at all of it. He burns the food, blisters his hands, asks questions about everything — not entitled questions, genuinely curious ones, and this is the thing that starts to move Vel's opinion from contempt toward something else.

Evening conversation over bad food. Kem asks why she spent. Not why she wants zero — why she started spending forty years ago. Vel tells him: she was seventeen, a girl was drowning in a canal, the Hoarded walking past wouldn't spend a single point on a rescue. Vel jumped in. That was her first level gone. She never stopped.

Kem tells her about his father. Not the political version — the real one. The empty eyes.

**State shift:** Real trust begins. Both characters become people to each other rather than symbols of opposing philosophies.

---

### Chapter 6 — "Sable Orm Has Never Left the Capital"
**POV:** Sable | **Engine:** Comic escalation / preparation

Sable Orm, level 300, Senior Auditor of the Hoarded Council, is assigned to retrieve Kem Aldraste. She protests: she's an administrator. She's told the Council can't deploy the Red Reserve — their secret unit of trained spenders — for a retrieval. Using trained spenders would publicly admit that inaction can't solve every problem. They need someone respectable. They need Sable.

She assembles five Hoarded agents, all level 300, all untrained. The team orientation is institutional absurdity at its finest: field protocols written by people who've never been in a field, equipment no one knows how to use, a map with annotations like "terrain manageable by foot." None of them have walked more than two kilometers in a single outing.

Sable's first ability use: she activates a Wayfinder to locate Kem. Information floods her senses — directional awareness she's never experienced. She has to sit down. Her Ledger reads 298. She stares at the number. The feeling is a violation, a release, and the beginning of something she can't name.

**State shift:** The pursuit is organized. Sable begins her transformation. The Red Reserve is seeded as a latent threat.

---

### Chapter 7 — "The Balance"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Discovery

Vel and Kem reach the Spent Towns — settlements where people average level 150-200. Farmers who use Fertility Charms, builders who use Shaping abilities, healers who spend on patients. Functional, self-governing, considered lower class by the Hoarded.

Kem has never seen working people up close. Not servants — people who labor because they choose to, who spend abilities to build rather than preserve. These people are more alive than anyone he grew up with.

They meet **Dren**, a trader at level 138 who runs goods between settlements. Dren knows the routes south and agrees to guide them to the Margins' edge — for payment, and because he's curious about a Hoarded lord burning down. Dren is practical, profane, and unimpressed by Kem's birth. He has a daughter, Mira, sick with something an expensive healing ability could fix. He's been saving levels for it. Helping Vel and Kem is a risk justified only by the payment.

**State shift:** The world expands beyond the Hoarded/Spent binary. Dren introduces the human cost of level economics.

---

### Chapter 8 — "The Professionals"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Action / comic escalation shifting to genuine threat

Sable's team catches them south of the Spent Towns. The fight begins as comedy: one agent activates a Binding ability and fuses himself to a boulder. Another unleashes a Perception Sweep so powerful it gives her a migraine. A third throws a Combat Pulse that detonates a grain silo.

Then it stops being funny. Raw power is raw power. An untrained level-300 agent swings a Strength-augmented fist and the air concusses. Trees snap. Vel takes a glancing hit from a misdirected Force Push and cracks two ribs. She burns four levels on combat and evasion — more than she'd budgeted for two weeks. Dren catches shrapnel from the exploded silo. Kem burns 42 levels on a defensive perimeter, overdoing everything.

They escape because the agents are too uncoordinated to pursue. But Sable watched the whole fight. She didn't engage. She learned.

**State shift:** Vel is forced below budget. Dren is wounded and invested. Sable begins studying combat. Incompetence at level 300 is catastrophically dangerous, and it's getting less incompetent.

---

### Chapter 9 — "Things He's Never Done"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Revelation / tender

Recovery. Kem tends Dren's wound — not with a healing ability, but with field medicine Vel's been teaching him. His hands shake. The stitches are ugly. Dren says: "That's the worst field work I've ever seen, but my arm's still on, so thanks." It's the first time anyone has thanked Kem for something he did badly.

Kem is at 178. He's lost more than a third of his total levels in less than two weeks. He takes stock: his Combat abilities have thinned, his Utility roster is shrinking, and the abilities he has left feel precious in a way they never did when he had everything. This is what Vel has lived with for forty years — the constant arithmetic of what you can afford.

Vel tells him about level zero. Not why she's going — just that it exists. That it's a threshold, not a death. That there's something on the other side: a place where people live without the Ledger. Kem sits with this. A world without the Ledger. He can't tell if it sounds like liberation or annihilation.

**State shift:** Kem develops non-ability competence. The sub-Ledger's existence is revealed. The alliance deepens through shared vulnerability.

---

### Chapter 10 — "Old Debts"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Confrontation / emotional pressure

They reach the settlement where **Thessa** lives — level 78, Vel's former partner who stopped spending twelve years ago. Thessa ran deep routes with Vel for eight years before the fear caught up. She told Vel the spending was suicide. Vel left. They haven't spoken since.

Thessa is not happy to see her. She's built a life — a house, a trade, a careful existence where she spends maybe one level per year. She's also been visited by Hoarded representatives who know Vel is coming. They've offered Thessa economic security in exchange for Vel's route information.

Two confrontations: the spoken one (Vel asking for route information, Thessa resisting) and the unspoken one (twelve years of silence, the accusation that Vel chose spending over people who loved her). Thessa gives partial route information — enough to help but not enough to expose herself. The Hoarded representatives arrive before Vel can push further, and the group flees.

**State shift:** Vel's past catches up. The Hoarded's reach extends to social pressure. Thessa's fear-driven betrayal mirrors the central choice in reverse.

---

### Chapter 11 — "The Problem of Too Much"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Constraint-heavy ordeal

A river crossing where the bridge is out. Kem's instinct: spend an ability. Bridge Conjuration, Flight Charm, Water Walking — he has options at level 138. Vel says no. The river is high but not impossible. She wants him to cross it like a person.

Kem fights the river for two hours. Swallows water, slips on rocks, nearly goes under twice. Dren crosses in twenty minutes. Vel in ten. Kem crawls out on the far bank exhausted and furious. He wants to know why she made him do that.

Because abilities run out. Experience doesn't.

Intercut: Sable Orm, at 270 after weeks of pursuit, reports to Lady Aldraste via Communication ability. Lady Aldraste's instructions are cold and institutional: retrieve Kem, minimal force, minimal spend. Sable doesn't mention that she's been spending more freely, that pursuing has begun to feel less like an assignment and more like her first real work. She doesn't mention that she watched Kem fight and thought: he's terrible at this, and he's happier than anyone I've ever met in the Vault.

**State shift:** Kem learns restraint as its own kind of strength. Sable's loyalty begins cracking.

---

### Chapter 12 — "The Margins"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Discovery / dread

They enter the Margins — depleted territory where generations of people have been ground down by Hoarded exploitation. Indentured labor programs requiring ability use. Debt structures payable only through spending. Communities where the average level is 15-30. The people here didn't choose to spend. They were spent.

The landscape matches: depleted farmland where Fertility Charms were overused and never replenished, crumbling structures built fast and cheap on Hoarded contracts. The people warm to Vel but are hostile to Kem when they learn he's Hoarded.

They meet **Fallow** — level 4, a sixty-year-old spent down through three decades of field labor. Fallow knows things about near-zero. People vanish when they hit zero — not die, vanish. Everyone in the Margins knows this. Nobody talks about it because what's the point? Fallow has seen it twice: a person there between one breath and not there the next. Not dead. Elsewhere.

Fallow describes the geography: a place called the Scour, three days south, where the land drops into a vast erosion basin. That's where people vanish.

Dren is quiet through this chapter. He looks at Fallow and does the math on his own Ledger.

**State shift:** The moral weight of the Ledger system becomes concrete. The Scour is identified. Vel's romantic view of spending is challenged by the reality of involuntary depletion.

---

### Chapter 13 — "Level Thirty-One"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Revelation / confrontation

Moving through the Margins toward the Scour. Kem checks his Ledger: 35. By afternoon, after a minor spend, he's at 31. Vel's number. The number she started the book at.

He tells her. She doesn't look at him. "Then you know," she says.

The revelation: as they study the route, Vel explains the mechanism. The threshold doesn't open for anyone who hits zero — it requires a sustained cascade of spending in proximity. One person can cross alone, but the threshold is unstable. Two spenders approaching zero together, especially if one is descending from high levels, amplifies the effect. She's known this since she watched someone cross years ago — a single crosser, unstable threshold, barely held. She's been looking for a high-level spender willing to burn down alongside her to stabilize the crossing.

Kem goes quiet. Then: "So that's what I am. A battery."

The confrontation is controlled but devastating. Kem doesn't shout — he's Hoarded enough to run cold when angry. He asks whether any of the last two weeks was real. The teaching, the conversations, the trust. Vel can't answer immediately, and the delay is its own answer.

They don't separate. They're too deep in the Margins and neither can survive alone at these levels. But the alliance is fractured. Kem walks ten feet ahead of her for the rest of the day.

**State shift:** The mechanism is revealed. The alliance cracks over Vel's manipulation. Kem passes through Vel's starting level and understands depletion.

---

### Chapter 14 — "Sable Orm at Two-Forty"
**POV:** Sable | **Engine:** Confrontation (three-way)

Sable finds them in the Margins. She's at 240, having spent 60 levels in pursuit. She's also the most changed character in the book — six weeks ago she'd never left a desk. Now she's a competent tracker, a passable combatant, and a person having an identity crisis.

She arrives alone — she sent her team back after the Chapter 8 fight. Three of the five were psychologically unable to handle further spending. The other two were becoming dangerous as they improved. Sable is alone.

The confrontation isn't physical. They sit in the ruins of a Margins farmhouse and talk. Sable asks Kem directly: why zero? Kem tells her about his father — the Stillness, the empty eyes. Sable knew Lord Aldraste. She's seen the Stillness. She knows he's right.

Sable makes a choice: she can't bring him back. Not because she can't overpower him (she has 200 levels on him) but because she understands that returning him would be putting him in a box to watch him rot, and she's spent too much of herself to pretend that's acceptable.

She doesn't join them. She's not ready. But she stops pursuing.

Before she leaves, she tells them the Council has authorized the Red Reserve. Trained spenders. People who actually know how to fight. They'll be at the Scour in days.

**State shift:** Sable becomes a person rather than a force. The pursuit transforms. The Red Reserve threat activates.

---

### Chapter 15 — "What She Can't Say"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Revelation / tender

Vel is at 14. A day from the Scour. She and Kem haven't spoken beyond logistics since the confrontation. Dren left two days earlier — his arm healed badly, his Ledger down to 125, his daughter waiting. Their farewell was brief.

Kem stops walking. He says he's not taking another step until she tells him why. Not the mechanism. Why she, specifically, needs to reach zero.

Vel tells him. Not smoothly — in pieces, with stops and starts and a long silence.

Maren. A woman she loved. They spent alongside each other for eleven years. Maren reached zero first, eight years ago, in an unplanned moment during a fight in the Margins. The threshold opened — Vel saw it, saw Maren there and then not there, saw a flash of somewhere else: buildings, people, light. Then it closed.

Vel has been trying to follow since. She burned from 62 to 31 in the eight years after, looking for a way to stabilize the crossing. She found the cascade mechanism. She found Kem. She's not sorry for using him, because Maren is on the other side and sorry won't bring her back.

Kem: "What if she's not there?"

Vel: "Then I'll be at zero in a world without the Ledger, and that's still better than this."

**State shift:** Vel's motivation becomes personal and specific. The emotional core lands. Kem chooses to stay for new reasons.

---

### Chapter 16 — "Everything You Can Afford to Lose"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Constraint-heavy ordeal / survival

The Scour — a vast erosion basin, wind-scoured, the bones of the world visible in layered rock. This is where the threshold opens.

The problem: the Red Reserve is already here. Two operatives, levels 265 and 258, trained in combat. Unlike Sable's team, they know what they're doing. They're positioned at the basin's only navigable descent.

Vel at 14. Kem at 12. Between them, 26 levels — fewer than most people spend in a lifetime. Every ability is a last ability.

They plan, scout, and attempt a route through a collapsed mining tunnel. Kem uses a light ability (one level) and a structural reinforcement (two levels) to keep the tunnel passable. Vel uses a Perception ability to map the Reserve's positions. Every spend is felt as a terminal cost. The dark comedy is grim now — Kem jokes that this is what the Hoarded would call "optimal preservation" and Vel almost laughs.

They emerge on the basin floor. The Reserve spots them.

**State shift:** Every remaining ability becomes life-or-death. The comedy goes dark and desperate.

---

### Chapter 17 — "What's Left"
**POV:** Vel | **Engine:** Climax / constraint-heavy ordeal

The final confrontation. Vel at 8, Kem at 9. Two Red Reserve operatives move to intercept.

Unlike earlier fights, the Reserve agents are precise and brutal. They know what they're doing. They have 250+ levels each. The power differential is staggering.

Vel and Kem fight with what they have — not just abilities but forty years and six weeks of experience. Vel knows how ability-reliant fighters telegraph. Kem has learned economy of action. They burn through everything. Vel uses combinations she's planned for months — maximum effect from minimum cost. Kem makes three critical spends: a defensive burst (five levels) that saves Vel from a lethal strike, a precision disarm he would have botched three weeks ago, and a final push to get them both to the threshold point.

The cascade activates. Vel reaches zero. Her Ledger empties. The threshold opens.

She sees it: a world beneath the world. People. Buildings. Light without the Ledger's hum. She steps through.

Kem is at 2. The threshold is open but destabilizing. The Reserve is recovering. Two levels won't sustain the cascade alone.

Sable Orm appears. She followed them from the Margins — not to help, not to stop them, but because she had nowhere else to go. In the moment of crisis, she burns seven levels in a single burst. The threshold steadies.

Kem spends his last two points. Zero. He crosses.

**State shift:** Both protagonists cross the threshold. Sable's transformation completes in a single decisive act.

---

### Chapter 18 — "Below"
**POV:** Kem | **Engine:** Aftermath / discovery

Kem wakes in the sub-Ledger. No Ledger. No abilities. No stats. Just a body, a mind, and a place he's never been.

The sub-Ledger is not a paradise. It's a civilization built by people who have nothing — centuries of involuntary zeros, ground down by the Hoarded's system, who've built agriculture, medicine, governance, and community from scratch without abilities. Harder, simpler, and more human than anything Kem has experienced.

Vel is there. She found Maren. Maren is alive, older, changed — she's been below for eight years, building. The reunion is not a fairy tale. Eight years apart doesn't vanish. But Vel is there, and Maren is there, and that's the thing that matters.

Kem walks the sub-Ledger. He meets people. He eats food someone grew without abilities. He looks at his hands — just hands, no stats, no potential energy, nothing — and feels something close to peace.

But: the Hoarded know about the threshold now. Sable saw it. The Red Reserve saw it. The sub-Ledger's secrecy — its only protection — is gone. Book 1 ends with Kem looking out over this new world, knowing the old world is coming for it, and knowing that for the first time in his life he has nothing left to lose and everything to protect.

**State shift:** The destination is reached. The sub-Ledger is real and fragile. Personal quests resolve. The saga question opens: what happens when the Hoarded discover that zero is a door?

## Middle-Book Progression Map (Chapters 8–14)

**Chapter 8 — "The Professionals"**
- *What's new:* Raw level-300 power causes real damage even when misapplied; Vel's careful level-budget is blown in a single fight.
- *What stops being sufficient:* "We can stay ahead of the pursuit" — force, not just speed, is now required.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 7:* The Spent Towns must establish the human world beyond the Vault before that world's safety is shattered by Hoarded violence.

**Chapter 9 — "Things He's Never Done"**
- *What's new:* Kem develops non-ability competence (ugly field medicine); Vel reveals the sub-Ledger's existence.
- *What stops being sufficient:* Kem-as-liability — he's now contributing value the Ledger can't measure.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 8:* The fight's physical aftermath creates the conditions for Kem's growth (tending Dren's wound) and Vel's disclosure (shared vulnerability after shared danger).

**Chapter 10 — "Old Debts"**
- *What's new:* Vel left people behind; the Hoarded use social pressure, not just force; Thessa's fear is rational.
- *What stops being sufficient:* Vel-as-self-contained-loner — her spending cost other people, and those costs are presented as real grievances.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 9:* Vel's emotional opening in Ch 9 is required for her vulnerability in Ch 10 to register as genuine.

**Chapter 11 — "The Problem of Too Much"**
- *What's new:* Kem solves a problem without spending; Sable's institutional loyalty cracks under her own transformation.
- *What stops being sufficient:* "Spending solves problems" — restraint becomes a valid survival strategy.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 10:* Kem's willingness to accept discipline requires the humbling effect of watching Vel be humbled by her own past.

**Chapter 12 — "The Margins"**
- *What's new:* The Ledger's victims are visible and specific; the Scour is identified; Fallow names the disappearances.
- *What stops being sufficient:* "Spending is heroic rebellion" — involuntary depletion is the system's primary violence, complicating both protagonists' self-narratives.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 11:* The discipline Kem learned in Ch 11 is tested against moral complexity — restraint means something different when you see what forced spending looks like.

**Chapter 13 — "Level Thirty-One"**
- *What's new:* The cascade mechanism has rules; Vel planned for Kem as a component.
- *What stops being sufficient:* The alliance-as-partnership — it was always partly an arrangement Vel engineered.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 12:* The Margins' moral weight is needed before Vel's manipulation can be understood as complex rather than simply villainous.

**Chapter 14 — "Sable Orm at Two-Forty"**
- *What's new:* Sable's identity crisis reaches a decision point; the Red Reserve is deployed; the pursuit ends but the threat escalates.
- *What stops being sufficient:* Pursuer-as-obstacle — Sable is now a mirror of the protagonists' transformation.
- *Why it can't swap with Ch 13:* The Vel-Kem fracture gives the three-way confrontation its charge — Sable arrives to a broken alliance, not a united front.

## Supporting Character Pressure Map

### Sable Orm (The Pursuer)
**Independent pressure:** A model Hoarded citizen for decades — organized, obedient, unspent. Being forced to spend levels in pursuit is dismantling an identity she's held her entire adult life. She's discovering competence, physicality, and the pleasure of action, which threatens her family, her position, and her self-understanding. Every level spent is a betrayal of the life she built around preservation.
**What she carries beyond plot function:** Whether it's too late to become a different person. She's spending involuntarily at first, then choosing to — and the line between coercion and freedom is the thing she can't name.
**Material chapters:** 6 (introduction), 8 (learning from combat), 11 (loyalty cracking), 14 (choice), 17 (decisive act).

### Dren (The Guide)
**Independent pressure:** A trader at 138 trying to save enough levels to afford his daughter Mira's healing. Helping Vel and Kem is a calculated risk — the payment helps, but every day on the road costs him time and levels he can't spare. His sick daughter is the math he does in every quiet moment.
**What he carries beyond plot function:** The arithmetic of a working parent in a system designed to extract from people like him. His judgments about Vel and Kem are sharpened by the fact that both of them are spending in ways he'd never be able to afford.
**Material chapters:** 7 (introduction, his terms), 8 (wounded by collateral damage), 9 (receiving Kem's first competent aid), 12 (quiet confrontation with the Margins' poverty).

### Thessa (The Former Partner)
**Independent pressure:** She stopped spending at 78 because she was terrified — she watched Vel accelerate toward zero and couldn't follow. She's built a careful, controlled life, and the Hoarded's offer of economic security is a lifeline she doesn't want to need. Her fear is rational: she's seen what spending does to people.
**What she carries beyond plot function:** The counter-argument to Vel's philosophy. What does it mean to choose preservation when the person you loved chose the opposite? Thessa's anger at Vel is real: Vel left her for spending, the way the Hoarded leave everyone for stillness.
**Material chapters:** 10 (central confrontation), 15 (referenced in Vel's Maren story — Thessa was part of that earlier world).

### Lady Aldraste (Kem's Mother)
**Independent pressure:** She loves her son and runs one of the most powerful Hoarded families. Kem's defection is a political crisis — other families question House Aldraste's discipline. She must pursue him publicly while privately being the person who watched her husband deteriorate into Stillness and knows, in a place she'll never admit to, that Kem saw what she's been trying not to see.
**What she carries beyond plot function:** The tragedy of the true believer who suspects the faith is wrong but has invested too much to leave.
**Material chapters:** 2 (present at Kem's departure), 11 (communication with Sable), 14 (referenced as force behind Council escalation).

### Fallow (The Depleted)
**Independent pressure:** Level 4, spent down through three decades of exploitative field labor. They represent the system's true product: not Hoarded wealth or the adventure of voluntary spending, but people ground to nothing by the machinery in between. Their view of zero is unsentimental — it's what happens to people the world used up.
**What they carry beyond plot function:** The moral correction to both protagonists' narratives. Vel treats spending as brave. Kem treats it as liberating. Fallow knows it can also just be what's done to you.
**Material chapters:** 12 (introduction, knowledge of the Scour), 16 (their geographic description guides the approach).
