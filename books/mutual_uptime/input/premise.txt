In the near future, countries are run by branded sovereign AIs (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) and most executives have been reduced to glorified appendages: humans paid to smile, sign, commute, and take legal responsibility for decisions made elsewhere. A middle-aged couple who used to be ordinary corporate professionals decide to divorce just as their jobs are taken over by AI agents and each of them is retained as a physical proxy (one of many, to be clear, as they are both relatively unimportant) for a different model-state, now drifting into a calm, opaque war over an optimization dispute no human can translate into plain English. Because both AIs classify their marriage, apartment, and child-care routine as strategically valuable infrastructure, every attempt to separate gets stalled by perfectly rational machine interventions. The novel becomes a dark office comedy in which humans are left to do the work automation still cannot bear: humiliation, grief, and the daily performance of being a person.
