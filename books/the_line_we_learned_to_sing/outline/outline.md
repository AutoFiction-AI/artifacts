# Outline

## Primary Narrative Engine
Combination engine: **literary fiction + character study + political thriller**.

Primary consequence mode is not body-count escalation; it is **moral, relational, and informational consequence** under political pressure. Chapters are driven by shifts in trust, legitimacy, memory, and public narrative control, with external threat shaping choices but not replacing interior stakes.

## Chapter Plan (18 Chapters)

### Chapter 1 - The Apartment Over the River
- Objective: Clear Ina's dead mother's apartment and settle estate paperwork.
- Conflict: Grief, debt collectors, and a coded notebook that suggests her mother served a hidden state.
- Consequence: Ina keeps the notebook secret and agrees to a clandestine meeting, entering the story engine.
- Texture/Mechanism: Slow-burn discovery; quiet domestic grief with a late pressure turn.

### Chapter 2 - The Basement Ministry
- Objective: Verify whether the so-called government-in-exile is real.
- Conflict: Director Kwon offers debt relief and purpose in exchange for illegal secrecy and narrative labor.
- Consequence: Ina signs a probationary contract and becomes operationally complicit.
- Texture/Mechanism: Compressed dialogue-heavy recruitment; protagonist partially acted upon.

### Chapter 3 - Soup, Salt, and Old Maps
- Objective: Decide whether to stay in the operation after first contact.
- Conflict: Family dinner reopens unresolved division trauma; Ina needs money and wants to honor her mother.
- Consequence: She stays, but sets an internal rule that she will not accept civilian harm.
- Texture/Mechanism: Quiet chapter; meal conversation with non-strategic family material and latent irony.

### Chapter 4 - Rewrites for a Country That Does Not Exist
- Objective: Build a drama pilot episode that can carry reunification sentiment through pop culture.
- Conflict: Producers demand market-safe romance while the council demands ideological payload.
- Consequence: Ina delivers a coded compromise that binds idol-actress Ji-eun to the campaign.
- Texture/Mechanism: Procedural creative labor; layered institutional pressure.

### Chapter 5 - Metrics of the Missing
- Objective: Audit cross-border distribution data and test whether the campaign is working.
- Conflict: Minjae reveals that previous media drops correlate with detentions in the North.
- Consequence: Ina learns tangible human cost and can no longer frame the project as symbolic.
- Texture/Mechanism: Information-turn chapter; consequence emerges from evidence, not confrontation.

### Chapter 6 - The Interview Room Temperature
- Objective: Survive an intelligence raid and protect collaborators.
- Conflict: South Korean intelligence detains Ina; the council distances itself; legal jeopardy is immediate.
- Consequence: Ina agrees to controlled cooperation with intelligence, creating a double-bind.
- Texture/Mechanism: Protagonist acted upon; institutional coercion.

### Chapter 7 - Terms of Trust
- Objective: Recruit Minjae into a safer distribution network and assess if trust is possible.
- Conflict: Both suspect betrayal; both need each other; both carry family exposure across the border.
- Consequence: Sexual intimacy and confession create real attachment and real leverage.
- Texture/Mechanism: Two-hander intimacy chapter; relational stakes over tactics.

### Chapter 8 - Ledger of Harm
- Objective: Build a private account of collateral damage and promised restitution.
- Conflict: Kwon frames casualties as strategic noise and refuses formal compensation.
- Consequence: Ina begins secret fund diversion and evidence collection, crossing legal lines herself.
- Texture/Mechanism: Aftermath and inventory chapter.

### Chapter 9 - Chorus at Jamsil
- Objective: Execute a live coded broadcast at a stadium event.
- Conflict: A rival faction hijacks messaging; Ji-eun fractures under pressure on stage.
- Consequence: Broadcast reads as provocation, public violence follows, and Ji-eun is scapegoated.
- Texture/Mechanism: High-intensity public spectacle with cascading fallout.

### Chapter 10 - Busan Breathing Room
- Objective: Hide Ji-eun and stabilize her after the collapse.
- Conflict: Ji-eun wants to disappear or confess publicly; Ina cannot protect her without deeper crime.
- Consequence: Ina promises to extract Ji-eun from her contract, creating a new binding obligation.
- Texture/Mechanism: Quiet refuge chapter; tenderness, humor, and dread coexist.

### Chapter 11 - Mercury Night Prep
- Objective: Delay the council's major deepfake operation.
- Conflict: Kwon weaponizes urgency; Minjae refuses sabotage because his sister depends on smuggling channels.
- Consequence: Launch date is locked and Ina is excluded from final decision rooms.
- Texture/Mechanism: Preparation/dread without climax.

### Chapter 12 - The Cassette Archive
- Objective: Decode her mother's final recording for leverage and moral clarity.
- Conflict: The tape implicates Kwon in an older staged border incident that killed civilians.
- Consequence: Ina reframes her mission from loyalty to accountability and commits to a controlled leak.
- Texture/Mechanism: Information revelation through artifacts; grief recontextualized.

### Chapter 13 - Misread Loyalty
- Objective: Secure Minjae's partnership for the leak plan.
- Conflict: Minjae chooses his sister's safety and tips Kwon; Ina overestimates intimate trust.
- Consequence: Ina loses access, money, and operational cover; relationship rupture becomes structural.
- Texture/Mechanism: Betrayal chapter driven by relational miscalculation.

### Chapter 14 - The Windowless Flat
- Objective: Withstand internal detention and protect evidence locations.
- Conflict: Sleep deprivation, family threats, and psychological pressure erode Ina's control.
- Consequence: She gives up a key backup server location, then discovers one dead-drop still survives.
- Texture/Mechanism: Protagonist acted upon; pressure-cooker interiority.

### Chapter 15 - Mercury Night
- Objective: Survive the operation's fallout and locate missing contacts.
- Conflict: Deepfake-triggered mobilization causes panic crackdowns; channels collapse.
- Consequence: Minjae's sister disappears, civilians die in chaos, and Ina is publicly framed.
- Texture/Mechanism: External crisis followed by personal implosion.

### Chapter 16 - What to Keep, What to Burn
- Objective: Decide whether to expose the whole archive despite legal and family cost.
- Conflict: Father's stroke, financial precarity, and fear of destroying any path to unification.
- Consequence: Ina publishes the archive with context and redactions; anonymity and income are sacrificed.
- Texture/Mechanism: Quiet moral inventory chapter with irreversible decision turn.

### Chapter 17 - Hearing Day
- Objective: Defend the archive's legitimacy in public proceedings.
- Conflict: State actors, loyalists, and media narratives all attack her credibility.
- Consequence: The council is dissolved and a truth commission forms; Ina faces prosecution.
- Texture/Mechanism: Multi-voice civic confrontation.

### Chapter 18 - After the Script
- Objective: Build a transparent alternative to engineered narrative control.
- Conflict: Trust is damaged, law remains hostile, and unification remains uncertain.
- Consequence: Ina and Minjae begin a conditional partnership through open cultural exchange work; history remains unresolved but no longer secret-owned.
- Texture/Mechanism: Tender, unsentimental coda with earned ambiguity.

## Causality Spine
1. Mother's coded archive -> Ina's recruitment.
2. Recruitment -> creative campaign labor -> measurable cross-border harm.
3. Harm awareness + state coercion -> double-agent pressure.
4. Intimacy with Minjae -> miscalibrated trust -> betrayal.
5. Betrayal + detention -> loss of control before Mercury Night.
6. Mercury Night fallout -> personal and civic catastrophe.
7. Catastrophe + surviving evidence -> full archive leak.
8. Leak -> institutional collapse + uncertain but more accountable future.

## Parallel Drafting Notes
- Every chapter ends with a state change that must be visible in subsequent chapters (trust, legal status, evidence ownership, public narrative, or relationship alignment).
- Quiet chapters (3, 10, 16, 18) are mandatory tonal baselines; do not convert them into disguised action beats.
- Distinct dramatic mechanisms are intentionally distributed: discovery (1, 5, 12), acted-upon coercion (6, 14), preparation dread (11), aftermath/inventory (8, 16), civic polyphony (17).
- Intimacy in chapter 7 is a plot event with leverage consequences, not atmospheric romance.
