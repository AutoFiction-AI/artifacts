# The Holdout Set

## Primary Narrative Engine
Dark comedy braided with literary fiction and character study. The book moves through humiliation, authorship theft, bureaucratic violence, erotic misreading, visa pressure, class shame, and shifting loyalties rather than thriller-style external peril. Its chapter consequences are primarily relational, professional, economic, and perceptual.

## Story Frame
At Haviland University in Lakebridge, New York, Leela Krishnan has entered what is supposed to be the final year of her PhD in the Civic Signals Lab. Her advisor, Mohit Saran, once sold the lab as a humane corner of machine learning: public-interest work, mutual aid, soft furniture scavenged from faculty offices, dinner after seminar. Then a summer grant loss and a secret startup deal harden him. He rebrands the lab around "adversarial robustness" and quietly assigns each student to attack another student's dissertation from the inside. What looks at first like ordinary academic cruelty becomes a system: contradictory promises, stolen figures, engineered compliance complaints, and a culture of scarcity so intimate it enters the bed.

The novel follows one academic year in which the lab's plural pronoun breaks apart. Leela is its central consciousness, but the book keeps testing the word "our": our advisor, our work, our future, our evidence, our shame.

## Core Cast
- **Leela Krishnan**: thirty, careful, long-stalled, ashamed of how much of her adult life has been spent in one basement lab.
- **June Park**: Leela's closest friend and harshest mirror in the lab; funny, exacting, privately tender, professionally ruthless when cornered.
- **Omar Elmasry**: theory-minded, visa-dependent, dignified until desperation makes dignity too expensive.
- **Ben Haber**: the newest student, eager for proximity to power, too flattered by attention to see the damage clearly until he is inside it.
- **Mohit Saran**: charismatic advisor whose turn toward startup logic and institutional self-protection makes him newly dangerous without ever making him melodramatic.
- **Gloria Fisk**: department chair, not evil but built for containment rather than justice.

## Chapter Plan

## Chapter 1 - Robustness
**Texture:** ensemble setup, faculty charm turning faintly sinister  
**Objective:** Leela wants formal approval for a final dissertation year and a clear path to graduation.  
**Conflict:** Mohit announces that every student will now complete an "adversarial" project that stress-tests another lab member's work under conditions of secrecy, and he makes Leela's approval contingent on participating.  
**Consequence:** The lab's old mutual-aid culture is structurally broken on page one; Leela consents because she cannot afford another year.

## Chapter 2 - Tuesday Dinner
**Texture:** quiet meal, jokes, omission, early mistrust  
**Objective:** The students try to restore normality at June's standing post-seminar dinner.  
**Conflict:** Everyone has clearly been promised something different by Mohit, but nobody wants to sound paranoid or disloyal by comparing notes outright.  
**Consequence:** Suspicion enters the room and stays there; Leela learns Omar has been promised a career-making slot that can only exist at someone else's expense.

## Chapter 3 - Root Access
**Texture:** solitary inquiry, low-light discovery, informational turn  
**Objective:** Mohit asks Leela to investigate an alleged repository breach, and she wants to prove herself useful enough to remain central.  
**Conflict:** The logs show Mohit cloned student work into a private directory linked to a company called Ruderal, but accusing him would also expose how dependent she still is on him.  
**Consequence:** Leela hides the evidence. Her first major mistake is not failing to see the harm but deciding to sit on it.

## Chapter 4 - Submission Window
**Texture:** deadline sprint, compression, crossed requests  
**Objective:** Leela must finish an adversarial evaluation paper before a major conference deadline.  
**Conflict:** Mohit keeps changing the metric, June needs the same compute resources, the data is unstable, and secrecy turns every request into a theft.  
**Consequence:** Leela pads a robustness claim with an unreplicated result and lets June's reproducibility problem worsen. The paper is submitted, but her own ethics and her friendship are damaged.

## Chapter 5 - Seminar
**Texture:** public humiliation, group dynamics under fluorescent light  
**Objective:** June wants to survive her internal seminar and preserve her authority in the lab.  
**Conflict:** Mohit has secretly positioned Omar's critique to detonate inside her presentation, then asks Leela to arbitrate in real time.  
**Consequence:** June is publicly diminished, Omar is implicated in the attack, and Leela's refusal to defend June becomes a relational wound that cannot be laughed off later.

## Chapter 6 - Buffet Logic
**Texture:** aftermath, cheap food, partial confession  
**Objective:** Leela and Omar try to understand what Mohit is doing without making themselves look weak.  
**Conflict:** Both want absolution more than they want accuracy; each admits only the portion of truth least likely to condemn them.  
**Consequence:** A provisional alliance forms between them, but it rests on selective honesty. The novel's coalition politics begin on compromised ground.

## Chapter 7 - Compliance
**Texture:** bureaucratic dread, protagonist acted upon  
**Objective:** Leela wants to avoid formal blame when Research Integrity summons her.  
**Conflict:** Mohit routed cloned repositories through her inherited admin credentials, and the university officer cares about audit trails, not mentoring dynamics.  
**Consequence:** Leela loses server access and her defense timetable becomes unstable. Academic harm turns legible as institutional record.

## Chapter 8 - Festival Lights
**Texture:** family chapter, domestic shame, temporary warmth  
**Objective:** Leela goes home for Diwali and wants one weekend in which she does not feel professionally fraudulent.  
**Conflict:** Her mother asks ordinary loving questions that sound impossible to answer honestly; Mohit's messages keep intruding; her family's competence exposes how suspended her life has become.  
**Consequence:** Leela lies about being on track and accepts money she needs but hates taking. The lab crisis now reaches into class and family shame.

## Chapter 9 - Baltimore
**Texture:** conference dislocation, hotel-night intimacy, hurt not resolved  
**Objective:** June and Leela need to salvage June's reputation at a regional conference while figuring out what Mohit is doing off-panel.  
**Conflict:** The presentation lands badly, June confronts Leela about earlier silence, and then June finds Ruderal's pitch deck using their unpublished figures.  
**Consequence:** They discover Mohit's theft in concrete form, and that night they sleep together. The intimacy changes their leverage and vulnerability without healing the earlier betrayal.

## Chapter 10 - The Deck
**Texture:** information chapter, forensic comparison, no climactic confrontation  
**Objective:** Leela and June try to determine whether the deck can prove misconduct rather than merely suggest opportunism.  
**Conflict:** Mohit can plausibly claim that lab figures belong to the lab; dates, versions, and authorship boundaries are messy; Ben has started hovering too close to every conversation.  
**Consequence:** They identify Ruderal as Mohit's secret company and realize Ben has become an accidental surveillance channel. Suspicion sharpens into strategy.

## Chapter 11 - Single Travel Slot
**Texture:** competitive ensemble pressure, scarcity formalized  
**Objective:** Each student needs Mohit's spring funding announcement to break in their favor.  
**Conflict:** Mohit says the lab has money for only one conference slot and one priority defense path, turning ordinary ambition into a controlled contest; Omar's visa dependence makes neutrality impossible.  
**Consequence:** Omar manipulates a baseline to strengthen his paper, Leela notices and says nothing, and the whole lab crosses another moral threshold together by pretending not to see.

## Chapter 12 - Expiration
**Texture:** administrative emergency with raw human stakes  
**Objective:** Omar needs a signed funding letter before his immigration paperwork lapses.  
**Conflict:** Mohit withholds the signature as leverage, Gloria Fisk wants the matter kept quiet, and Omar's panic makes every conversation jagged.  
**Consequence:** Leela ghosts part of Mohit's letter and bribes Ben with prospective authorship to get the document uploaded. She saves Omar for now by acting more like Mohit than she wants to admit.

## Chapter 13 - The File
**Texture:** preparation and dread, document-gathering without release  
**Objective:** June, Leela, and eventually Omar assemble a grievance dossier strong enough to survive institutional minimization.  
**Conflict:** The file only becomes convincing if Leela includes the log evidence she concealed in chapter 3 and the padded result from chapter 4, which would implicate her as well.  
**Consequence:** She hesitates at the last minute and submits a weakened version. June sees that Leela still wants justice without full self-exposure.

## Chapter 14 - Forwarded
**Texture:** betrayal, cascading email fallout, offstage violence becoming immediate  
**Objective:** Once Ben forwards the draft grievance to Mohit, the students need to contain the damage before Mohit reframes everything.  
**Conflict:** Mohit moves first with professionalized language about misconduct and offers June an industry introduction if she disentangles herself from the others.  
**Consequence:** June takes the meeting and goes silent. Ben becomes more frightened and more useful to power. The lab's fragile coalition breaks.

## Chapter 15 - Committee
**Texture:** closed-room ambush, body-level collapse after procedure  
**Objective:** Leela wants her pre-defense meeting to give her a date, a room, and a future.  
**Conflict:** Mohit presents her as derivative and ethically compromised using the very conditions he engineered, while the committee rewards his composure over her history.  
**Consequence:** Her defense is postponed indefinitely. The career she thought she could still salvage is abruptly no longer there.

## Chapter 16 - January Inventory
**Texture:** winter quiet, laundromat and frozen lake, aftermath and decision  
**Objective:** Leela must decide whether to leave, go silent, or finally burn the version of herself built for institutional survival.  
**Conflict:** Shame argues for disappearance; rent argues for caution; June is half gone into interviews; no official process promises justice anyway.  
**Consequence:** Leela names and encrypts the full evidence archive as `holdout_set` and gives it to the union lawyer. For the first time, she chooses a course that may ruin her further but is not primarily about saving face.

## Chapter 17 - Open House
**Texture:** public set piece, messy exposure, no clean triumph  
**Objective:** The former lab members coordinate a controlled release of evidence during Haviland's industry demo day, when sponsors and faculty are in the same room.  
**Conflict:** Mohit remains brilliant at framing exploitation as innovation, Gloria wants containment, Ben wavers, and June must decide whether to corroborate publicly after having taken the meeting in chapter 14.  
**Consequence:** June backs the evidence, Mohit is placed on leave, and Ruderal's deal freezes. But the lab's publication pipeline, recommendation network, and internal trust also collapse with him.

## Chapter 18 - Holdout
**Texture:** spring coda, diminished future, wounded plural pronoun  
**Objective:** The survivors have to decide what of the year can be carried forward and what must simply be endured.  
**Conflict:** No official finding uses the words the students would use; June leaves for industry, Omar's enrollment remains precarious, and Leela's salvaged path is smaller than the life she imagined.  
**Consequence:** Leela submits a stripped-down dissertation under a provisional arrangement and starts a one-year teaching fellowship. Privately, the former lab keeps the encrypted folder and the memory of a plural they can no longer speak casually. The holdout set is what the institution failed to absorb.
