In an alternate 1898 river metropolis run on fermented algae, a foul-mouthed sign painter becomes famous for naming the city's proliferating new products, street strains, and hybrid pests, though nobody can agree which inventions are genuine and which are just advertising made flesh. When a runaway glow-moss starts changing color according to whatever the public calls it that week, he is pulled into neighborhood feuds, patent hustles, and an improbable romance over who gets to name a city remaking itself in slime.
