During Deathly Hallows, while Snape runs Hogwarts, the Carrows brutalize students, and Death Eater attacks keep turning the castle into a war zone, a mediocre fifth-year Hufflepuff follows a footnote in A History of Magic into a restricted-section manuscript that shouldnt exist and discovers the First Art -- a substrate beneath all magic that doesnt cast spells but edits the rules that make spells possible -- along with proof that the ancient civilization that created it didnt die but left, and that its wards around Earth are failing against something that regards wizards the way wizards regard flobberworms. Nobody believes him, so he works alone, treating detentions, curses, and eventually the Battle of Hogwarts as increasingly irritating distractions from the real work. By the final act he has barely begun to understand the First Art, but what little he has grasped is enough to end Voldemorts war in seconds -- he simply doesnt, because it is not worth the interruption.
